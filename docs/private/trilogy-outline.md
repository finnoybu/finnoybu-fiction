## Trilogy arc in three sentences

1. A Norwegian farm-and-coast boy of eighteen goes to sea knowing only that he loves the island-girl Olava, and discovers across successive voyages that his body answers also to certain men he meets — an attention he has no word for, no tradition to place it in, no safety to name it.
2. At sea he survives tyrannical captains and rotten ships; at home he almost dies of tuberculosis; what saves him is the slow integration of a double knowledge — what Olava gives him of love, and what men at sea give him of desire — until by twenty-four he can recognize, in private, what he did not even suspect at eighteen.
3. He marries the woman he loves, and he does so with the newly-acquired private knowledge that the full map of his affections is larger than the one he can ever show her, and that carrying that cost unshown is the price he chooses to pay to be honest to both halves of what he turns out to be.

**Craft note — self-discovery, not self-knowledge:** Olav at 18 does not know he is multisexual. He does not arrive at the dock in Stavanger with a category or a language for what will happen to him. Book I is about the first private recognitions that he does not yet know are recognitions — the body-noticing, the attention, the unasked-for grief when a particular man leaves. Book II is about the discovery itself becoming articulate enough that he can think it, though not say it. Book III is about the crystallization of what he has become, at the point where he chooses Olava with full knowledge of the other half of himself he is not renouncing but carrying. The Miller register's reserved physicality and body-noticing is the ideal tool for this — the voice can render the attention without naming it, and then can render the naming, and then can render the carrying.

## Voice register at a glance (per [voice-register.md](voice-register.md))

- **Olav → Miller** (*Song of Achilles*, *Circe*): literary-restrained, body-noticing, queer-awakening, classical cadence.
- **Olava → Tóibín** (*Nora Webster*, *Brooklyn*): interior, quiet, grief-and-waiting, domestic spaciousness.
- **Haakon → Proulx** (*Brokeback Mountain*, *Shipping News*): rough-Scandinavian-sailor rural, weather-on-the-tongue, elliptical pain.
- **Jens Hestby → Robinson** (*Gilead*): elegiac paternal, theological, letter-like contemplation.
- **Sven Haugen → Proulx** (same family as Haakon — see "Casting questions" below — distinct characterization).
- **Thoresen → Hemingway** (one aside chapter only, "The Photograph"): terse, unsaid, held.
- **Anders, Thomas:** never-POV. Power of unknowability preserved.

Emotional-mirror architecture:
- **Haakon arc:** joy in Book I, absence in Book II, grief in Book III.
- **Olava arc:** distant-waiting in Book I, grief in Book II, joy in Book III.
- The two lovers' opposite trajectories cross at Book III's apex.

# BOOK I — Spring 1875 → Christmas 1877

*Title working:* **Salt and Silence** (per CLAUDE.md).
*Length target:* **~115K words, ~30 chapters** (≈380–400 pp at literary-fiction density, within Ken's 360–440 pp range). Olav 18 → turning 20 on a Wilmington sickbed.

## Book I structural beats

1. **Act I — The boy who goes out (Ch 1–8, summer 1875 – spring 1876).** Olav at 18, sent as cook on *Nanna*; seasickness, first hierarchies, first taste of maritime authority. Returns to Finnøy for winter with his father Jens Hestby and younger brother Peder. The inland-farm/coast tension (Jens as Robinson-register father whose Lutheran contemplation grounds the home while Olav strains toward the sea).
2. **Act II — The tyrant (Ch 9–16, spring 1876 – winter 1876).** *Faina* voyage with Capt G.K. (named in fiction: **to be decided — memoir uses initials only**). Stavanger to Shields to Lisbon and back. Eliasson's death falling from the yard. The Finnøybu identity forged against the captain's cruelty. First encounters with men in ports. **Haakon introduced** — the Hogganvik boy-sailor, first voyage like Olav, builder of the ship — working relationship deepens into the first private recognition. *This is the book's joyful pole.*
3. **Act III — The island summer (Ch 17–20, June–July 1876).** Finnøy between voyages. The Landa gaard Sunday where Olava runs over and grabs his arm. The walk home through the ravine at twilight. Stavanger courtship. Photograph exchange. Olav's father Jens witnesses Olav's divided heart without knowing it divides. Departure on *Hjemmet* late July. **Haakon and Olava — the two knowledges — now both planted.**
4. **Act IV — The great voyage (Ch 21–26, August 1876 – November 1877).** *Hjemmet* under good Captain Tollefson — the counterweight to G.K. Archangel, the Atlantic crossing, Jamaica's logwood months, the hurricane-crossing back to Bristol, the tar-mat punishment in Wilmington. Throughout: letters between Olav and Olava (the proposal from Bristol, the acceptance at Cape Verde). **Haakon reappears** at sea — on *Hjemmet* or via port-crossing, cast TBD (see Casting questions below). *Haakon-joy peaks here in a long ports-and-passage interval.*
5. **Act V — The wound (Ch 27–28, Nov–Dec 1877).** Wilmington desertion with Thomas. The O'Brien Catholic Irish family. Mrs. O'Brien's nursing. Mary Ellen (13) and Margit (9) tending "Willie" after the boiling-water attack and the country-fever delirium. First Christmas away from home. **Book I closes as Olav boards the *Cito* for Hamburg three days before Christmas 1877, still bandaged, left leg partially paralyzed, and with Mary Ellen's embroidered bookmark in his pocket.**

## Book I sourced/invented ledger

**Entirely sourced (plot spine):** *Nanna*, *Faina*, *Hjemmet*, voyages, crew rosters, Eliasson's death, Lisbon incidents, proposal-and-acceptance letters, desertion with Thomas, O'Brien nursing, the scalding, Mary Ellen's bookmark. Do not alter these facts. Date-shifts from memoir timelines should be minimized.

The logwood-loading Jamaican "Tom" as a port encounter. Captain Tollefson (memoir real) becomes the good-captain counterweight.

**Invented wholesale:**
- **Haakon's interior life and voice.** His Proulx register. His emergence as Olav's first lover and the intensity of Book I's male pole.
- **Jens Hestby's POV** (Olav's father). His Robinson-register letters or reflections interlayered with Olav's sea-voyages. A small number of Jens-POV chapters — probably two in Book I.
- **Olava's interiority as POV** — Tóibín-register chapters rendering her half of the courtship and the long six-month wait for her "yes" to reach him. Probably three in Book I.
- **The queer content** — body-noticing, private recognition, the weight of not-saying, the erotic specifics. The memoir is silent on all of this; fiction supplies it from the register.
- **Olav's fully-shaped interiority** across all of the above.

# BOOK II — Christmas 1877 → autumn 1879

*Title working:* TBD (CLAUDE.md flagged).
*Length target:* **~105K words, ~28 chapters** (≈360–380 pp at literary-fiction density). Olav 20 → 22. *Most interior of the three books* — tuberculosis scare, first real reunion, dying-and-healed arc — but nine major movements (*Cito* / Hamburg / NY slaveship / Bordeaux / homecoming / nav school / TB / *Allegro* / *Diana*) support the length easily.

## Book II structural beats

1. **Act I — The floating coffin (Ch 1–5, Christmas 1877 – February 1878).** *Cito* from Wilmington. Six-week hurricane voyage pumping day and night. Mid-Atlantic prayer at the anchor windlass. The water-tank fails; the miraculous rain fills empty tanks in winter at sea. The young lad from Hamar who can't hold himself upright — Olav lifts him again and again. Arrival at Hamburg; ship sinks a foot at anchor the first night after crew leaves.
2. **Act II — The slaveship and its opposite (Ch 6–10, February – midsummer 1878).** American bark hires Olav in Hamburg for NY return. The 1st mate and the Red Shirts 2nd mate; the Austrian steward; the butcher-knife Frenchman; the international cruelty. Olav and the one-eyed Dane **stay through the desertion** at NY to collect wages. Then the overnight transformation — new captain, new officers, international brotherhood, the old Stavanger-resided-NY steward feeding them hotel meals for three weeks. Voyage to Bordeaux. **Twin dudes in Bordeaux** — francs, a fine boardinghouse, new suits, umbrellas as canes, the American captain dipping the national colors three times in goodbye. Hired onto the Sandefjord ship to Norway.
3. **Act III — The homecoming (Ch 11–14, midsummer 1878).** Olav at 21, returned after 2 years 3 months. Meeting Olava at Stavanger: she is 19, fully grown, "no longer the little girl." Roalsøy and her family: the father (notebar fisherman, ageing), the mother (friend of Olav's dead mother in youth), Gustav the teasing brother, the three married brothers on their divided pieces of Roalsøy. Olav's own home on Finnøy, brother Peder, father Jens. Summer 1878 — "the happiest days of my life."
4. **Act IV — The school and the sickness (Ch 15–20, October 1878 – April 1879).** Navigation school in Stavanger. Room at Kindingstad's on Bredalmendingen. Borrowed boats to Roalsøy nights. Skating at Mosvannet. Bethania Hall with Lars Oftedal. The cough develops. Dr. Due: *not yet, but it may come*. The memory of dead siblings. Silence with Olava. April 1, 1879 — exam passes. Olav has the diploma but no health, no money, no readiness to marry.
5. **Act V — The disaster and the mercy (Ch 21–24, June – September 1879).** The fishing trip with Peder (midsummer light, eider eggs, two brothers side by side). *Allegro* — Vardøy, tight-fist captain, Tobias from Finnøy on first voyage, gray-gray-gray country, bad-cold collapse. Doctor confirms. Discharge fought for and won. **Then *Diana* — Captain Sars.** The inside-coast home journey in July and August, with green slopes after weeks of gray, the Bible-reading older crewman, fatherly care, 200 kroner earned, cough vanished without his noticing when it left. Home to Stavanger September 1879. **Book II closes on Olav at Roalsøy, fully healed, empty-handed, watching Olava do her evening family worship by candlelight — "what the fifth attempt will not lose."**

# BOOK III — Autumn 1879 → January 22, 1882

*Title working:* TBD.
*Length target:* **~125K words, ~33 chapters** (≈420–440 pp at literary-fiction density, top of Ken's range — this book carries the heaviest plot density and the trilogy's closing emotional spine). Olav 22 → 24.

## Book III structural beats

1. **Act I — The great voyage east (Ch 1–10, October 1879 – October 1880).** Olav hired onto *Semiramis* via Helland. Ceremonial departure — first-class steamer Stavanger→Hamburg, rail to Flensberg, 15-man crew. **Haakon-return.** The Hogganvik boy Olav hasn't seen in 3½ years now a man; their reunion aboard is Book III's first major beat. Shields coal; cross-equator ritual with the Farsund steward; waterspout at Olav's helm; Flying Dutchman mirage near the Cape; Rangoon approach with the golden pagoda rising from the sea; three months in Burmese heat; the 80 coolies; the sawmill elephants; the Sunday strike; the "Verdensposten" letter to Olava on 30×36-inch rice-paper. Return via Madagascar, the Cape, St. Helena, Ascension telegraph home, scurvy at Falmouth. Copenhagen arrival with captain's proud storm-entry, Trekroner landing drenched, sea-legs at the hotel. **The Haakon joy-pole of Book I returns here transformed — not as first-discovery but as claimed intimacy — and then is lost.** (Cast: how Haakon exits. Desertion at some port; accident; quiet muster-off at a foreign consul. Memoir has no anchor for this, so it's wholly invented, and it sets up Book III's grief.)
2. **Act II — The winter graveyard (Ch 11–14, October – December 1880).** Olav takes 2nd mate papers on *Olivier* of Drammen — 42-year-old ex-French-man-of-war brig. Copenhagen to Sundsvall for lumber. The ice-crusted rigging. The frozen feet in cavalry boots. The Bornholm near-wreck. **The Skagen-light night** — Olav 8 hours at the wheel in snowstorm with topgallants standing; 120 ships wreck in Kattegat the next morning; their rotten brig alone survives. **Haakon is dead or gone by now** — the news reaches Olav at Leith or Greenock. *Haakon-grief peaks.* Olav hires onto *Favorit* for Cuba and warmth.
3. **Act III — Sugar and the woman in the painting (Ch 15–20, December 1880 – July 1881).** *Favorit* under Capt Reinertson. Biscay tornado tears all sails to rags in 20 seconds. Three weeks sewing new sails in trade-winds. Cienfuegos — rotting streets, Florida-Water perfume to send home. 580 hogsheads of brown sugar; Olav and the mate eat it by the bowlful. Boston. **The oil-painted portrait of Olava** — the middle-aged painter comes aboard with a sample, 10×12 inches on a metal plate, masterwork frame. Olav gives him Olava's newest photograph. The finished painting will outlive both of them and still sit on Olav's wall in 1939 while he writes the memoir weeping. *This is the hinge of the trilogy — the moment Olav knows exactly what his fidelity is for.* Return to Karlshamn via Atlantic 30 days easy sailing. Muster-out. Coast-steamer to Copenhagen.
4. **Act IV — The Swedish encounter (Ch 21–26, August – November 1881).** Hired onto *President Daae* of Tvedestrand as **1st mate at 22, highest mate's pay in Norway**. Captain Thoresen (Hemingway-aside-chapter subject). Copenhagen→Malmö steamer. **The Swedish figure on the promenade deck** (see Casting below) — the straw-hat exchange, the quiet conversation, the brother-in-NY address, the agreed letter from Stralsund. Passage to NY with the German steward's club-attack and conversion to utter loyalty. Brooklyn under the new bridge (now higher above the East River). All crew desert except the steward. **Two letters arrive:** Olava's ordinary and warm, and the Swedish figure's declaration. Olav writes the "diplomatic letter." Petroleum cargo, Hoboken. Second-mate goes mad at midnight — the chase with one oar and the opsang-sung hoist back aboard bound. Captain's week-long absences. **Helsingør, early November.** Second pair of letters. Olava: come home, I have waited long. The Swedish figure: flaming declaration; will come anywhere Olav says; wants marriage. **Olav's silent cutoff.** Discharge negotiated around the cordage debt. Danzig petroleum run, beer-bribed unload, the drunken broker's-clerk overboard-in-an-armchair incident.
5. **Act V — Homecoming and the cracked mug (Ch 27–30, December 1881 – January 22, 1882).** Storm off the Norwegian coast; the Tvedestrand pilot jumps from his half-submerged boat tied to a rope while his 15-year-old son steers the boat through breakers. Safe in home harbor. **Captain Thoresen's farewell three days of parties** — his unmarried sister presented, Olav wary after the Swedish cutoff. **The Hemingway-register "The Photograph" aside chapter** — Captain Thoresen's interior with his cracked white-and-blue-striped coffee-mug. The superstition that is also a grief. What Thoresen knows about Olav and what he will never say. Coast-steamer to Stavanger. Olav at Roalsøy's door. Two years three months again. **Wedding January 22, 1882.** The final Tóibín-register Olava chapter: *joy.* The final Olav chapter: Olava and the painted portrait of herself on the wall, and in his chest the closed room where the Swedish figure will live forever. News a month later: *President Daae* lost on Jutland. *Haakon-grief still lives here, underneath.* The trilogy closes on Olav holding Olava and knowing — in the Miller register — what the cost of being whole has already been.
