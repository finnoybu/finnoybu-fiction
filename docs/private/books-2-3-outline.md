# Books II & III — Outline (landing-zone shaped)

Drafted by forward-writing session 2026-04-19 as scaffolding for drafting Books II and III. Derivative of `trilogy-plan.md` (read-only). Where this outline contradicts the plan, the plan wins; flagged contradictions noted at the bottom.

**Revision 2 (2026-04-19, same session):** Three structural revisions per Ken's review.
1. *Book II Act III pacing.* Reduced from 11 to 9 chapters (Ch 22–30); each chapter now carries new tension rather than transitional motion. Two chapters added to Act II (now Ch 11–21): a Calcutta city-days chapter at Ch 12 and a "morning after" silence chapter at Ch 19, giving the heat scene proper aftermath breathing room.
2. *Anders restrained.* The Copenhagen meeting is recognition only; Anders's letter at Ch 28 is parallel-echo to Haakon's three-line Shields letter (brief, formal, short of saying what cannot be said). The architectural beat is the *doubling* of letters in the drawer, not a second heat payoff. Sven at Ch 18 carries the book's heat alone.
3. *Book III heat scene relocated.* From Ch 2 (wedding night) to Ch 21 (homecoming after the two-year *Daae* voyage), where the marriage has been tested by absence. Wedding night at Ch 2 stays tender and present but is no longer the peak.

**Path B chronology recap.** Olav born March 1858. Book II opens June 1881 (age 23, departing Stavanger on the *Semiramis*) and closes December 1882 (age 24, back at Finnøy, pre-wedding). Book III opens spring 1883 (age 25, wedding at Hestby) and telescopes through later years to a late-life beat. The plan's "20–21" and "21–22" ages reflect the pre-sweep chronology.

**Shape match.** ~30 chapters per book, ~3K words/ch avg, ~85–90K words/book, three-act, dual POV sustained.

**Outline depth.** Chapter title + one-line terminal beat. Not a beat sheet. Prose is free to surprise itself within these landing zones.

## Book II — *Finnoybu: The Monsoon Letter* (working subtitle)

**Arc.** Olav sails on the *Semiramis* to Calcutta and Rangoon as common seaman; rises to recognized navigator under Captain Brun; encounters Sven the Bergen crewmate (the queer-charged echo of Haakon, this time with Olav as the older/competent one); takes the peak heat scene at a Calcutta lodging-house; returns via Copenhagen, where he meets the Swedish man Anders; receives Anders's letter on the same morning as Olava's at Finnøy after his return; adds Anders's letter to the workbench drawer alongside Haakon's. Ends pre-wedding, December 1882.

**Architectural notes for the writing:**

- Dual POV continues. Haakon thread: 2 chapters (Ch 4 outbound, Ch 24 homeward), echoing Book I Ch 4.
- Sven POV: 1 chapter (Ch 16), placed between the bunk-fight and the heat scene.
- Olava POV: 2 chapters (Ch 9, Ch 20) — Lindøy as she receives letters; her ledger device sustained.
- Heat scene at Ch 18 (Outlander-tier explicit, single scene, earned). The thing Book I deferred. **Anders is restrained** — his Copenhagen scene is recognition only, his letter is parallel-echo to Haakon's three-line Shields letter, and the architectural beat at Ch 28 is the *doubling* of letters in the drawer, not a second heat payoff. The Sven scene at Ch 18 carries the book's heat alone.
- Drawer expands by one folded sheet at Ch 28; the drawer is now load-bearing as a recurring physical fact across all three books.
- Hestby church motif: brief beat at Ch 30 (the churchyard the day before the wedding); the parish priest who reads the banns has known Olav since baptism.
- **Pacing weight.** Act I: 10 chapters (outbound, getting to India). Act II: 11 chapters (India, weighted toward the heat-scene aftermath at Ch 19 *The Morning After*). Act III: 9 chapters (homeward + the letter); each chapter carries new tension rather than transitional motion. Telescoped chronology: 18 months across 30 chapters, weighted toward India and the homecoming sequence.

### Act I — Outbound (Chs 1–10)

1. **Open Water** — first day at sea on the *Semiramis*; Sundberg's discipline; the bark settling into her course
2. **The Forecastle** — eighteen common seamen; Sven introduced (Bergen, ~24, married, child); his hammock five down from Olav's
3. **The North Sea** — first storm; Olav's chart-eye watching Brun take a noon sight from the deck
4. **[Haakon POV] At the Tyne** — Haakon at Shields, hears from a Bergen sailor that Hestby is on the *Semiramis*; he does not write a second letter; the not-writing is the form
5. **Biscay** — Olav teaches the youngest common seaman to read a chronometer; the mentor instinct activates
6. **The Cape** — coal, water, refit; first foreign port for Olav as a competent man; he registers the difference between the boy at Wilmington and the man at Cape Town
7. **The Royal Yard** — Sven and Olav aloft together; small noticing; the body returns to attention after two years of holding it for Olava
8. **The Indian Ocean** — long passage; private chronometer lessons to the steward in exchange for tobacco
9. **[Olava POV] The First Letter** — Lindøy late autumn 1881; the Cape Town letter arrives; her ledger gets a paragraph
10. **The Hooghly** — Calcutta arrival; the river pilot; the city's noise; timber discharge begins

### Act II — India (Chs 11–21)

11. **Chart Corrections** — Olav with Brun ashore in Calcutta; he is the navigator Brun bet on; small intoxication of competence
12. **The Hooghly Days** — Calcutta the city across multiple days; Olav as navigator on duty and as foreigner off it; the timber discharge proceeds; first long ashore-time he has ever had in a non-European port
13. **The Shwedagon** — Rangoon; the gold pagoda; Olav's interior breaks open at a foreign sacred place
14. **The River** — near-drowning swim in the Rangoon River; Sven pulls him out; body-on-body recovery on a sand bar
15. **The Bunk-Fight** — Sven and Olav's charged confrontation in the forecastle, ostensibly over a stolen knife; the crew pulls them apart
16. **[Sven POV] What Sven Knew** — Sven's interior; the wife and child in Bergen; the recognition; the fear
17. **The Apes** — apes caught by their own grip on coconuts; Olav watches; the metaphor lands without being named
18. **The Lodging-House** — peak heat scene. Two nights ashore in Calcutta while the bark is in dry-dock for hull repair. Single scene. Earned. Both men know it cannot continue.
19. **The Morning After** — silence between Olav and Sven the next day; the work goes on; the not-speaking is a third thing in the room with them; both men understand it can never be spoken of and never repeated
20. **[Olava POV] The Second Letter** — Lindøy spring 1882; the Calcutta letter arrives; shorter than the others; the postscript about a Swedish crewmate is the kind of thing Olav writes when he is not writing the thing on his mind
21. **Outbound for Home** — Rangoon clears the bar northbound; the homeward leg begins; the work fills the days the lodging-house cannot

### Act III — The Letter (Chs 22–30)

22. **The Cape, Returning** — winter Atlantic homeward; Brun confirms the mate's berth offer for the next *Semiramis* voyage; at Saint Helena Olav drafts a letter to Olava with *there is a thing I must tell you when I am home* and tears it up
23. **The Steward's Confidence** — homeward leg; the steward Olav has been teaching navigation says he will leave the sea after this voyage; Olav, for the first time aloud, articulates his own intention to come ashore after this is done
24. **[Haakon POV] At Shields, Still** — Haakon learns from a Norwegian sailor at the customhouse that the *Semiramis* is northbound through the Channel; he weighs writing again; he writes nothing
25. **Copenhagen** — the bark calls for orders before continuing to Bergen; Olav ashore for two evenings; meets Anders at a quayside tavern; not physical; recognition only; Anders walks him back the second evening, asks for an address; Olav gives Finnøy. After: a midnight watch in the Skagerrak with a single thought he cannot put down — he gave Anders the address.
26. **Paid Off at Bergen** — promotion confirmed; First Mate's berth offered for the next *Semiramis* voyage in a year; Olav declines; Brun reads him correctly without saying so
27. **Lindøy in November** — Olav arrives at the same jetty he left from eighteen months before; Olava in the wool coat; the accumulated ledger between them; the thing he must tell her stays in his throat
28. **The Two Letters** — both letters arrive on the same morning at Finnøy. Olava's is in the kitchen. Anders's is in Olav's hand at the boat-shed. **Anders's letter is in the same hand-restraint as Haakon's three-line Shields letter** — brief, formal Swedish, short of saying what could not be said. The doubling is the architectural beat; the letter does not carry the heat (the lodging-house already did). Olav opens the workbench drawer. Haakon's letter is still folded inside. He folds Anders's once. He puts it in the drawer. Two letters now.
29. **Christmas at Lindøy, Again** — quieter than the 1879 Christmas; Olava and Olav at the back garden bench at midnight; she does not ask; he does not tell; the not-asking is the engagement-becoming-marriage. She notes (without saying) that there is more in the drawer than there was.
30. **The Churchyard** — the day before the wedding; Olav at the Hestby churchyard; his grandparents' stones in the period spelling; the parish priest who reads the banns aloud has known Olav since the baptism; the accumulated weight; closes on the eve and the work to come

## Book III — *Finnoybu: The Captain's Burden* (working subtitle)

**Arc.** Wedding at Hestby spring 1883. Brief settled period; Olav takes a first mate's berth on the *President Daae* (petroleum, New York/Brooklyn) to fund the household. The Brooklyn drama: Bowery near-murder, Peder Gjesdal the gangster, the steward who attacks him and becomes devoted, Captain Thoresen's cruel-to-kind transformation. Returns. Captain's drunkenness at the homecoming dinner; Olav's outburst; the captain discharged. Olav decides to come ashore for good. Years on, the Anders thread closes — Olav reads of Anders's death in a Stockholm shipping notice. Years later again, a Bergen paper carries Haakon's death notice. Telescoped late-life beat: Olav in middle age at the Hestby churchyard with his own name in the period spelling on family stones. Bittersweet, not triumphal. The drawer in the workbench is opened twice more in his life: once to add Anders's clipping, once to read Haakon's. Olava never asks. Olav never tells. The not-telling is the marriage.

**Architectural notes for the writing:**

- Dual POV continues. Haakon: 1 chapter (Ch 17, Bergen quay). Thoresen: 1 chapter (Ch 10, mid-Brooklyn). Olava: 3 chapters (Ch 6, Ch 14, Ch 26) — increasingly load-bearing as the marriage takes form.
- No new male love interest in Book III. Anders's death is reported, not shown. The choice has been made; the cost is paid by being lived with.
- **Heat scene at Ch 21, not Ch 2.** The wedding night at Ch 2 is tender and present but not the peak — a real first night, brief, in the loft at Finnøy. The peak heat scene is reserved for **Ch 21 *The Loft, Again*** — Olav's homecoming after the two-year *Daae* voyage, when the marriage has been tested by absence and distance. The marriage proves itself in the body at the moment it has earned the right to. Sustains the multisexual frame: Olav loves both, has chosen Olava, and the loving-of-Olava is itself a real thing the prose has to render — at full craft bar, not gestured at.
- Hestby motif arrives at full weight: wedding (Ch 1) and the late-life churchyard beat (Ch 29). The name closes around the life.
- Telescoped chronology: Chs 1–22 cover roughly 1883–1885 (wedding, the *Daae* voyage, the homecoming, coming ashore). Chs 23–30 cover ~20 years (settling, children, the death notices, the late-life beat). Pacing pivots at Ch 22.

### Act I — Marriage and the Berth (Chs 1–9)

1. **Hestby** — the wedding at the church on Finnøy; the period spelling on the parish register; Olava's hand on his face the fourth time
2. **The Loft** — wedding night at Finnøy; the first night of the marriage; period-appropriate intimacy; tender, brief, present. **Not the peak** — that is held for Ch 21.
3. **The Year At Home** — spring through summer 1883; Olav at the boats with his father; Olava setting up the household; small marriage-formation moments
4. **The President Daae** — autumn 1883; first mate's berth offered; Captain Thoresen's reputation; Olava agrees
5. **The Outbound** — departure from Stavanger as a married man; the second goodbye of his life; Olava does not come to Stavanger this time
6. **[Olava POV] The Empty House** — Olava at Lindøy and Finnøy through autumn 1883; the ledger continues
7. **Atlantic Crossing** — the bark runs west; Olav as first mate; the work is different from common seaman's work
8. **Brooklyn** — arrival; the petroleum wharf; the city; first impressions
9. **The Bowery** — Olav lost in the city after dark; near-murder; the gangster Peder Gjesdal; rescued by a Norwegian-American who recognizes the accent. The gangster and the rescue are folded into one chapter; the city has rooms a sailor does not enter twice.

### Act II — Brooklyn and Thoresen (Chs 10–18)

10. **[Thoresen POV] What Thoresen Knew** — Thoresen's interior; why he is cruel; what Olav has begun to soften in him without trying
11. **The Steward** — the steward attacks Olav over a bottle of whiskey; Olav subdues him
12. **The Devotion** — the steward, after, becomes Olav's shadow; the masochistic inversion of the *Sigrid* captain
13. **The Wharf at Night** — second Brooklyn winter; Olav writes the longest letter home he has ever written; sends it
14. **[Olava POV] The Long Letter** — Lindøy receives the long letter; she reads it three times; her ledger gains a section called *the things Olav has begun to tell me*
15. **Thoresen Changes** — the captain's cruel-to-kind turn; small, accretive; Olav notes it without commenting
16. **The Petroleum Run** — return cargo; the bark southbound for Stavanger
17. **[Haakon POV] Bergen Quay** — Haakon, now a captain himself of a small Bergen-Tyne coaster, reads in a paper that the *President Daae* is due in; he does not go to Stavanger to meet her; he carries on
18. **Homeward** — the final crossing; Olav at the wheel in a North Atlantic storm; the body-knowing of fifteen years at sea

### Act III — Coming Ashore (Chs 19–30)

19. **The Homecoming Dinner** — Stavanger; the captain, drunk, says the thing about the men he tried to kill (echo of *Sigrid* Captain Gjermund); Olav's outburst — *you tried to murder men;* the captain's discharge by the company the next week; Olav's decision to come ashore
20. **Olava at the Quay** — she has come this time; the wool coat is older; the kiss is no longer the first
21. **The Loft, Again** — homecoming intimacy at Finnøy after two years apart. **The peak heat scene of Book III.** The marriage has been tested by absence and distance and proves itself in the body. Period-appropriate explicit, married register; not the lodging-house's transgression but the harder, quieter thing — wanting your wife after two years and being able to say so without words. The multisexual frame at full craft bar.
22. **The Boat-Shed** — the morning after; Olav at the workbench; the drawer is the same drawer; nothing has been added since Anders's letter; he does not open it; he stands at the workbench
23. **The First Land Year** — 1885; Olav buys a small share of a coastal smack with his uncle; he is not at sea but he is on the water; he can do this
24. **A Daughter** — first child born; named for Olav's mother; the family begins
25. **The Newspaper Notice** — five years on (early 1890s); a Stockholm shipping notice in a Bergen paper; Anders's name among the lost on a Baltic steamer that went down in November; Olav reads it twice; he does not tell Olava
26. **[Olava POV] What She Has Always Known** — Olava in middle age; the ledger has been closed for some years; she has not asked; she will not ask; the not-asking is the marriage
27. **The Drawer Revisited** — the children are nearly grown; Olav at the workbench; he opens the drawer once, to add the newspaper clipping with Anders's name circled, and closes it again
28. **Haakon, In a Paper** — a Bergen paper carries a notice of Captain Haakon Berg, lost at sea in a North Sea gale; Olav reads it on a Tuesday morning; he goes down to the boat-shed and stands at the workbench for an hour
29. **The Churchyard** — late life (1900s); old Olav at the Hestby churchyard; his grandparents' stones; his parents' stones; the period spelling of the name carved on every one of them; the encounter with the name
30. **The Dedication** — final chapter; the gathering at Hestby for the baptism of a grandchild; the priest reads the name aloud; Olav signs the parish register; the name closes around the life

## Open questions / where the prose may push back

- **Sven's name and merger.** Memoir uses Sven for a Bergen crewmate; novel uses it for the queer-charged crewmate. Could split into two characters (Sven Senior the older friend; a younger Sven the queer charge) or keep merged. Defer to the prose.
- **Anders's surname and trade.** Sketched as a Stockholm-bound steamer officer or merchant's son; the right specific identity will surface when his scene is being written. Don't lock the surname now.
- **The telescoped end of Book III.** Chs 23–30 cover ~20 years. This is not Book I's chapter-pace. The final third may need re-paced as it's drafted; some chapters may compress further (covering 3–5 years each) and others may not appear at all.
- **The Hestby late-life beat.** Plan's "old Olav at the churchyard" is sketched at Ch 29 here. Could be the very final beat (Ch 30 instead) or a distinct epilogue. Prose-time decision.

**Resolved in revision 2:**
- ~~Anders letter restrained vs. passionate~~ → restrained, parallel-echo to Haakon's three-line Shields letter. Book II's heat is carried by Sven at Ch 18; the letter at Ch 28 is architectural (the doubling), not a second peak.
- ~~Heat scene in Book III placement~~ → Ch 21 (homecoming), not Ch 2 (wedding night). The marriage proves itself in the body at the moment it has earned the right to.
- ~~Book II Act III pacing risk~~ → Act III reduced to 9 chapters, each carrying new tension; freed chapters moved to Act II (Calcutta city-days at Ch 12, morning-after silence at Ch 19) where they give the heat scene proper breathing room.

## Contradictions with `trilogy-plan.md` (read-only)

- **Ages.** Plan: Book II 20–21, Book III 21–22. Path B: Book II 23–24, Book III 25+. Path B governs.
- **Captain of the *Semiramis*.** Plan calls him Hamre; Book I Ch 22 names him Brun (Hamre is the navigation school classmate). Brun governs.
- **Wedding date.** Plan: January 22, 1882. Path B: spring 1883. Path B governs.
- **Marriage location.** Plan locks Hestby. Confirmed by Path B sweep on Ch 21 (engagement language softened to "the parish church the parties choose"; Hestby is the chosen church).

These are flags, not edits. The plan-of-record stays as written; this outline notes where it is now superseded.
