# Memoir digest — *A Sailor's Reminiscences from the Days of the Sailships*

> **Purpose:** Structured extraction from the source memoir for trilogy drafting. Read before outlining; consult during chapter work.
> **Source:** `d:/dev/sea-reader/content/en/` (68 chapters, .md with `id` frontmatter). Combined renders: `d:/dev/sea-reader/output/combined.md`.
> **Author:** Olavus Vullum Bjørnson Vestbø (signs as "O. W. Vestbo"). Born early 1860 (age 15 summer 1875; age 19 at navigation exam April 1879; age 22 at marriage January 1882). Memoir completed Payallup, WA, April 8, 1939.
> **Citation convention:** `Ch N / slug` — e.g. `Ch 11 / youth-and-amor`.
> **Fictional age shift:** +3 years applied in trilogy. Memoir Olavus at 15 → fiction Olav at 18. All dates in this digest are memoir dates; the outline doc applies the shift.

---

## 1. Author's frame and voice

- Self-deprecating but proud; acknowledges his own rebelliousness (the "Finnøybu" identity — named after home island Finnøy, used pejoratively by Captain G.K. and adopted by Olavus himself).
- Explicitly promises to write events as they happened, "without regard to favorable or unfavorable reflections as to myself" (intro).
- Charges his wildness partly to "my Viking ancestry" — desire for freedom, hatred of bondage (intro).
- Religious: quietly devout Lutheran, prays privately in crisis (Ch 26 at anchor windlass during the *Cito* voyage); attends Bethania Chapel with Olava; notes providence repeatedly through life.
- Notes his weight twice: 203 lb in 1877 (England, age 17); 220 lb in 1878 (age 18). Lacked an inch of six feet at 16. Large and strong — repeatedly succeeds at feats of strength others couldn't.
- Late-life frame: writes in tears about Olava (Ch 31 parenthetical: "Now, I sit here, aged and alone, dreaming and writing…"). Closes memoir on what would have been her 80th birthday.

## 2. Chronological spine — voyage by voyage

### Pre-voyage
- **Born early 1860**, Vestbø farm on [Finnøy](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Finn%C3%B8y), west-coast Norway. Mother died "many years earlier" before age 16 (Ch 2); mother was from Rossøy near Finnøy, "very beautiful, and of a quiet, lovely nature" (Ch 31). Father still living in 1876; brothers and sisters (some already dead of tuberculosis per Ch 32).
- Youngest brother **Peder**, 2½ years younger (Ch 33 — present only in the navigation-school interval fishing trip).

### Voyage 1 — *Nanna*, brig of Stavanger — summer 1875, age 15
- **Role:** cook. **Duration:** 6 months. **Route:** Baltic and North Sea.
- 1st mate: **Paul Hjelm** (brother of Abraham Hjelm, later mate on *Allegro*).
- Suffered seasickness; learned steering, sail handling, splicing.
- *Ch 1 / the-first-lessons.*

Winter 1875–76 at home.

### Voyage 2 — *Faina*, three-masted schoonership — spring 1876, age 16
- **Role:** youngman (ordinary seaman, one of two "youngmen"). **Route:** Stavanger → Shields (coal) → Lisbon (salt) → Stavanger. Departed March.
- **Ship name** revealed Ch 4. Owned partly by **John Stensøy** (cousin of Olavus's father; lived near Stavanger; believed married to relative of Olavus's mother — so the voyage felt "like sailing on a ship belonging to our family").
- **Captain "G.K."** — elderly, weather-beaten, pock-marked, unkempt hair, sprawling thin full beard, big red nose, bushy eyebrows, broad-shouldered, "seadog", rough voice, stammer. Refined quiet wife at home in Stavanger. **A drunkard, tyrant, and coward; tries to kill Olavus at least twice.**
- **Crew (11 total):** 1st mate (middle-aged, reddish full beard, sour, grouchy); 2nd mate **Karl Oberg** (young, full-grown, friendly, good-natured; later resided in Portland OR, died a few years before memoir written); boatswain from [Hogganvik](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Hogganvik) (helped build ship, ~25, small and thin, first-class fellow, first voyage, became Olavus's good friend; parted at Stavanger and lost touch); sailor **Thompson** (tall, thin, quiet, reserved — reappears as boatswain later in chapter; tall/thin/taciturn; reappears years later on *Semiramis*); sailor **Eliasson/Elisøson** from [Lysefjord](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Lysefjord) (~25, stocky, red cheeks, gentle, "would not offend even the ship's cat" — **falls from yard, dies**); youngman **Nils** from Hogganvik (~25, slow, stammer, not a natural seaman, sympathetic); steward/cook from [Flekkefjord](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Flekkefjord) (~30, big black full beard, easy to get along with); plus Olavus and a deckhand.
- **Events** (critical for Book I):
  - *Ch 2 / coming-home* (note: misleading title — this is the **departure** of *Faina*): hiring visit to Capt G.K.'s home; captain's friendly wife; dinner; inspection; crew signed at registry. Trust betrayed almost immediately. Rotten rye-crust bread, olive-oil ration, salt meat "horsemeat and mahogany", 5-inch-thick pork, no vegetables.
  - *Ch 3 / the-bay-of-biscay-in-uproar:* English Channel gale; captain strikes Nils with fists, kicks him; **Eliasson falls** while reefing upper topsail (gums mittens, hands slip, breaks both legs and gets hole in forehead on anchor chain). His last words: "Please, captain, do not jerk my foot so hard." Buried at sea wrapped in canvas with coal in bottom of rude coffin — 2nd mate Karl Oberg sings a hymn, captain at helm roars at Olavus for lowering "like a (terrible execration) cow", second mate's tears. Twelve days without fresh water — tank leaked seawater. Near-collision with another schoonership in Biscay night — two ships parted by providential wave.
  - *Ch 4 / the-proud-captain:* Lisbon (Tagus river, fort at mouth, marble-paved streets with inlaid stars). Olavus and Nils left on shore by drunk captain 12 hours; captain beats Nils unconscious on half-deck, kicks him. Olavus hides in mizzen-rigging, sleeps with marlinspike. Captain tries to capsize ship during salt loading (hold empty, deck loaded one-side only), bombards Portuguese stevedores with salt-lumps; Portuguese attack with boathooks. Captain tries to kill Olavus in hold with salt shovel — Olavus ducks, shovel shatters on iron post. Mate nearly kills steward with flung brass teapot embedded in roof beam.
  - *Ch 5 / an-english-man-of-war:* Five English warships arrive for the Crown Prince of Sweden-and-Norway's visit; flagship 5-masted with yards on 4 masts, 900 crew, biggest sailship Olavus ever saw. 21-gun salute nearly deafens.
  - *Ch 6 / captain-able-seaman:* Palm Sunday morning — captain botches anchor-chain lashing, whole anchor and 45 fathoms to bottom; dredge grabhook borrowed from the English warship. Captain botches chain-stopper, chain flies over rail and he's thrown about.
  - *Ch 7 / punishment-for-disobedience:* Rotten-pork grease job at mizzen masthead. Captain braces royal yard to make sail flap both ways, trying to knock Olavus off (nearly succeeds). Captain swings heavy rope-end at Olavus; rope bounces off stay, recoils into captain's own wrist.
  - *Ch 8 / no-holidays:* Alone tarring fore-rigging during his free watch. Spills tar on deck as protest. Captain rages helplessly. **After this, captain never touches Olavus again.**
  - *Ch 9 / dead-reckoning:* Captain's navigation so poor that dead reckoning places them "thirty miles up in the Norwegian mountains." Reach Stavanger. Crew cuts seizings to unbend sails fast despite orders.
  - *Ch 10 / home-coming-celebration:* Steward, carpenter, Olavus feast in captain's salon on captain's port wine. Next morning at customhouse unloading captain's smuggled orange crates and port wine, captain calls Olavus a cow; Olavus explodes publicly in front of **John Stensøy**, enumerating captain's crimes. Stensøy reports to principal owner. **Captain forced to beg pardon and join temperance society to keep his position.**

Summer 1876 at home on Finnøy.

### Summer 1876 — meeting Olava, Finnøy
- *Ch 11 / youth-and-amor.* Sunday afternoon gathering at **Landa** (gaard on Finnøy, not far from Vestbø, Olavus's home). Stavanger-dialect visitors from **Roalsøy**. Tag game ("tikken"). **Olava** — small-stature, light-complected, light blue dress, yellow straw hat, lively; slightly older companion is her brother's fiancée. Three months difference in age. Names mirror: Olaus/Olavus and **Olava**. Walk home via ravine. Olava runs over, grabs his arm: "Come, let's go." At Roda (gaard on NE Finnøy) they part; she ascertains when he'll be in Stavanger.
- Meets her in town within days. She's from **Roalsøy** near Stavanger. Has lost one brother to tuberculosis (nursed him) about a year ago. Attends [Hetland](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Hetland) parish — **Rev. Lars Oftedal** chaplain, old **Sagen** pastor.
- They photograph each other; no explicit talk of love but understood. Olavus sets his goal: navigation school, officer's papers.

### Voyage 3 — bark *Hjemmet* ("Home") — July 1876 to ~March 1878, ages 16–18
- **Role:** ordinary seaman, then able seaman. ~20 months.
- Hired via shipping-master **Helland** (long neck, well-known Stavanger character — reappears as broker through Olavus's career). Owned by **Heling company**.
- **Captain Tollefson** — originally from Sand, now Stavanger. Middle-aged, friendly, agreeable. **A fine man and excellent singer**. Plays organ in his cabin, sings hymns ("Holy Darling", "Sweet Belle Mahone", "Darling, I am growing Old", "Sweet By And By", "I Walk In Danger All The Way"). **The anti-Captain-G.K.** — the captain who treats men well, to whom Olavus later deserts with terrible regret.
- **Crew:**
  - 1st mate: middle-aged, heavy brown full beard, mostly agreeable but capricious and hasty. Later strikes **Thomas** (see below) in face so blood flows.
  - 2nd mate **Pallesen**: ~30, good-natured, too conscious of dignity, ingratiating to captain.
  - Carpenter **Bertel** (from [Tau](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Tau,_Norway) near Stavanger, ~25, just married — wife took parting hard). Olavus's closest friend on this ship.
  - **Theodor Halversen**: son of Captain Halversen of old *Harald Haarfagre* (pitch-pine trade, Pensacola). 17–18, wild but hale fellow, well-met. Reappears later as classmate at navigation school.
  - Boatswain: 45+, stooped, thin, wrinkled, jolly except when called out at night; big eater; excellent opsang-starter.
  - Steward: old man, married to a woman much younger, hired out of charity by captain, sent home after one voyage.
  - Unnamed youth from [Jelsa](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Jelsa_(village)) — "first-class comrade," later became a student, "one of my best friends."
  - **Thomas** from [Stjernerøy](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Stjernar%C3%B8y)/Finnøy (did farm work at Finnøy before this voyage). Wild chap. Has a Finnøy girlfriend (former schoolmate of Olavus). **Deserts with Olavus in Wilmington winter 1877.**
  - Nine men in forecastle.
- **Ship:** "extraordinary craft" — built in Bremerhaven for Australian passenger trade, oak throughout, very strong; upper/hurricane deck lowered 3–4 ft aft so pilot house doesn't protrude; patented blocks and tackle; "like a gull" even in the worst storm; best-handling ship Olavus ever sailed.
- **Route:** Stavanger → [North Cape](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/North_Cape,_Norway) → [Archangel](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Arkhangelsk) (load lumber) → [Cardiff](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Cardiff) (unload) → [Wilmington NC](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Wilmington,_North_Carolina) (ballast) → loaded resin → 19-day hurricane crossing to [Bristol](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Bristol) → Cardiff (coal) → [Madeira](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Madeira) → [St. Vincent, Cape Verde](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/S%C3%A3o_Vicente,_Cape_Verde) (coal) → [Jamaica](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Jamaica) (logwood) → [Goole](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Goole), Humber River (unload) → dry dock classified Star A-1 → ballast → Wilmington NC again → **Olavus deserts Ch 24** — autumn 1877.
- **Critical moments (rich adaptation material):**
  - *Ch 12 / to-archangel:* departure; Archangel's "Russia-smell," black bread with salt herring baked in, one-way river lighters, gray huts among tree-stumps.
  - *Ch 13 / my-first-voyage-to-america:* rotten foregallant mast-top discovered while rigging national-flag signal.
  - *Ch 14 / boyish-carelessness:* orange thrown at boatswain's open mouth mid-heave-ye-ho. Fight with boatswain in forecastle; Olavus pins him; afterward friendship. Later fights with other older men all end the same way.
  - *Ch 15 / bill-and-his-negro-boss:* Black driver singing all day to Black horse named Bill at horse-power derrick in Wilmington. Standalone vignette.
  - *Ch 16 / crossing-the-atlantic-in-a-hurricane:* 19 days Wilmington → Bristol, mostly under bare rigging; steersman's lapse while watching mate and carpenter at mizzen mast, near-broach. Bristol Channel pilot **prays on deck on his knees** before they enter. Tug dickers £100 → £60. Chaotic night docking at Bristol: hawser breaks, ship smashes pier sending sparks, collisions with smaller ship (takes its bowsprit), collision with big steamer (foreshrouds tangled in anchor), pilot with 15-inch-edge axe cuts stays. Ship reaches mooring looking like a wreck. Feb 1877.
  - *Ch 17 / impressions-of-bristol:* Old man **James** distributes tracts and books, takes sailors to Good Templars' Hall (temperance society). Letter from Olava. Ballast-shifting crisis outside Cardiff at night when wind reverses.
  - *Ch 18 / in-tropical-seas:* Cape Verde / St. Vincent. Coal unloading. Young Black boy dives for captain's thrown sixpence. **Letter from Olava delivered here**: her "yes" to his proposal (posted from Bristol). *"Pop the question from the west coast of England, and be accepted on the west coast of Africa."* Now betrothed.
  - *Ch 19 / jamaica-tropical-paradise:* The "tropical paradise" chapter. Old barefoot limping Negro pilot ("Me pilot") and his son steer through breakers with every sail set. Six weeks of logwood loading (July–Aug 1877). Lizards pour out of tree hole. Tom the singing helper. Negro Charley calling from his hut at dusk. Local bamboo huts and woven walls. Palm-climbing. English clergyman and two grown daughters visit twice. Shark warning saves Olavus's feet in shallow pier water. Green land crabs in grass. Broad-fruit and saw-sop fruit. Sugar mill with rum extraction. Moved to second bay by thundershower.
  - *Ch 20 / sailing-in-the-gulf-stream-with-scorpions:* Scorpion on hatch tarpaulin almost strikes his bare arm. Tarantula in forecastle evades every blow. Gulf Stream science-musings.
  - *Ch 21 / worms-in-the-sea-bisquits:* Old Cardiff sea-biscuits worm-infested; worms floated off on hot coffee by skimming; carpenter eats without removing.
  - *Ch 22 / threatening-clouds:* First mate learns his name is Bjørnson, calls him **"bjørn" (bear)** as provocation — Olavus resents. Tests him by having him carry a rock no one else could lift; Olavus succeeds ("It isn't bear for nothing").
  - *Ch 23 / low-pressure:* Captain Tollefson whistles at mizzen mast for wind after a calm; gale comes that evening. Later: "The powers in nature are not to be mocked." Main topgallant brace broken deliberately (Olavus + carpenter pulling hard together). 1st mate strikes **Thomas** in face; Olavus bites back rage.
  - *Ch 24 / a-fight-for-existence:* Dispute with 2nd mate loading cordwood at 6pm from another Norwegian ship; 2nd mate promises aloft-work torture at sea. Files complaint with **Norwegian Consul, Wilmington** — demands muster-off. Consul can't discharge because Olavus has no complaint against the captain. Mates retaliate with unstool-able tar-mat and tar-forestay jobs. Olavus takes down all mats and re-tars and reseams without stool using a haliard loop. **Deserts with Thomas** with help of a Swedish cook and a sympathetic steward. 11:30 pm night escape with chests into boat via lighter.

### Wilmington winter 1877 — the O'Brien Irish family
- *Ch 25 / ups-and-downs-of-a-deserter.* Hidden in an old brick building behind the boardinghouse the first two days. Boardinghouse kept by **O'Brien** family (assumed name; Catholic Irish).
  - Mr. O'Brien: not a good businessman, boozes, mostly harmless.
  - **Mrs. O'Brien**: able housekeeper, manages the house; competent amateur doctor (saves Olavus's scalp, then cures his "country fever" with epsom-salts purge).
  - Grown son, works in a city store, lives at home, fine young man.
  - **Mary Ellen, 13** — face, form, poise, intelligent; embroiders a bookmark "to Willie. Welcome. Mary Ellen"; later receives a $2 sewing case as parting gift.
  - **Margit, 9** — lively, mischievous, drinks the sugar-dregs of his tea cup. Receives a smaller gift.
  - The girls call him **"Willie"** (couldn't pronounce Olaus; he says Vullum → Willie).
  - Five Irish millworker boarders (hard drinkers); two other Norwegian sailor lads (one 16, on first voyage); a Swedish cook.
- **The attack:** Irish tailor buys 16-year-old Norwegian boy's blue Scotch cap for $2; savage-looking Irishman palms a 50¢ piece; Olavus exposes him; Irishman returns, finds Olavus alone in the rear shanty (cook house) by the stove, attacks with fist → Olavus kicks him against a table → Irishman grabs kettle of **boiling water** and empties it over Olavus's head. **Scalp, forehead, back of hands burned.** Mrs. O'Brien bandages with white lard, later salve. Irishman jailed. **Olavus refuses to testify** (fears the ship's captain will find him and reimpress him).
- **Country fever** two weeks later — purging cure.
- **The girls nurse him daily with soups and candies.** "Real ladies, or, to me they were more like good angels." Margit pours him a second cup of tea without emptying the first — then they both run up and confess: "Now I know why Willie is so sweet, he uses so much sugar in his tea."
- Mrs. O'Brien charges $23 total: $21 at $1/day board + $2 for medicine and care.
- Left leg partially paralyzed from unconscious time lying on left side; knee joint affected.
- **This Catholic family has been a treasured memory with me all my life.**
- **Adaptation value:** This is some of the richest material in the memoir for a Book I or Book II emotional set-piece. A queer young sailor nursed back to life by Catholic girl-children of a family not his own. The tenderness is explicit; the memoir protects it from any taint. For the trilogy, this is also where Olav's need to both *be loved as a son* and *be protected in a feminine household* is set up — a counter-weight to the homoerotic possibilities at sea.

### Voyage 4 — bark *Cito* of [Mandal](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Mandal,_Norway) — Dec 1877 to Feb 1878
- **Role:** able seaman (wages now $30/month). **Route:** Wilmington NC → Hamburg, resin cargo (+100 barrel deck load).
- **Crew:** 13 men. Young lad on first Atlantic; man from [Hamar](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Hamar), ~30, congenial; carpenter from Stavanger, married to a Swedish woman, resides in Hamburg.
- **The "floating coffin."** Ship built in US of soft wood, oakum works out when hull flexes. Olavus helped two deserters from *Cito* escape so he could take their place unknowing.
- Christmas Eve gale in Gulf Stream — springs leak; 6 hours on / 2 hours rest pumping rotation for **six weeks straight**. Water tank leaked to salt — 2 days on pint rations before a **miraculous rainstorm at mid-Atlantic in winter** fills the empty tank. Deck cargo breaks loose one night; Olavus single-handed pitches barrels overboard. Cabin windows smashed by big sea, provisions half-soaked. Galley cooking gear washed overboard — coffee only for rest of voyage.
- **Olavus prays at the anchor windlass nightly**, in secret; believes God heard him. Cites Psalm 107:23–32.
- Ship pumps out a barrel every ~3 gallons per stroke, ~300–400 gallons/minute full crew. Men wear oilskins in bed. "Waterlogged feeling" akin to seasickness. Haakon-tier weather.
- Reach Hamburg Feb 1878 — **ship sinks one foot at anchor during the first night** after crew leaves; new crew must be hired to keep pumping.
- *Ch 26 / life-in-a-floating-coffin.*

### Hamburg 1878
- *Ch 27 / in-hamburg.* Swedish-family boardinghouse near the docks. *Cito*'s carpenter (Stavanger, married Swedish wife, residing Hamburg) hosts a visit home. Casino/dance pavilion — 50 musicians on arched bridge, 3000 dancing; Olavus seated at a table, "Never having learned to dance, I thought it was something of the most useless a person could do."
- Hamburg + Altona = "one of the biggest cities I had seen."

### Voyage 5 — American bark (unnamed), Hamburg → NY → Bordeaux — ~Feb to midsummer 1878
- **Role:** able seaman. 13 men, 7 nationalities. Hires via Swedish 1st mate (~45, big brown full beard, "grobian").
- *Ch 28 / a-pirate-crew.* **The slaveship.**
- **Crew (westbound leg):**
  - Captain: small, middle-aged, sickly, sour, taciturn, **consumptive**, American (only American aboard). Home near Boston.
  - 1st mate: Swedish, ~45, brown full beard.
  - **2nd mate** (NYC "Red Shirts"): Norwegian from [Lillesand](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Lillesand), but resided NY 12 years. Over seven feet tall, broad, strong as a giant, "rude and more vulgar than a wild beast." Always wore a thick red flannel shirt outside his pants. Feared by NY police.
  - Steward: Austrian, small, easily irritated, malicious, dangerous.
  - Boatswain: German, reserved, silent, not bad.
  - Middle-aged French seaman: cudgel and saber scars (split thumb), dangerous; carries an 18-inch butcher knife.
  - Danish seaman, one-eyed, "likeliest comrade."
  - Very young Englishman.
  - Little Swede (25) — adopted as cabin-boy by 1st mate (fellow Swede).
- **Food:** "hogfeed" pea soups several days old, potato peelings with pork-rind bristles, salt meat boiled in seawater. Captain's "medicine" when sick: "Drink this [epsom salts], and then go to work, and work as long as you live, and when you are dead I shall have you thrown overboard."
- Frenchman threatens Olavus with knife for speaking Norwegian in forecastle: "Not a word but English to be used here. Look to your safety."
- Steward vs 2nd mate confrontation (boiling water / wet-deck kick / butcher knife / revolver) — both desert that night.
- **All crew deserts at NY anchorage except Olavus and the one-eyed Dane.** They stay to collect wages. Boardinghouse runners with revolvers press them; they refuse.
- Olavus sees the **Brooklyn Bridge** under construction — "145 feet over the East River" — "the wonder of the world."
- *Ch 29 / festive-days-in-a-slaveship:* Overnight, a new regime — new captain (American, Boston homeport), new 1st mate (Finnish), new 2nd mate (German), new steward (an old Stavanger steward resided NY 15 years, serves hotel-quality meals for three weeks while ballast out / petroleum loaded at Staten Island). International brotherhood of five nations in forecastle (2 Hollanders, 1 Scot, 1 Englishman, Dane, Olavus). Olavus called **"Bill"** on this ship.
  - **Wharf-rats night:** Olavus, the Dane, and the little Swede go to Bowery dance hall; Dane gets drunk, misses last ferry to Staten Island with them. Sleep rough on ferry pier planks; two suspicious men stand over them twice; walk all night.
- Voyage to Bordeaux — 2.5 months. Fine captain. He asks them to help make newer men feel welcome on his ship.
- *Ch 30 / two-dudes-in-bordeaux:* Midsummer 1878. Captain begs them to stay: offers all their money, five days ashore on full pay, then re-sign. They insist on going home. Discharged with special notations for ability and good deportment — 400–500 francs each. Stay at a very fine 5-francs-per-day boardinghouse, bottle of port wine per meal. Full new suits, hats, umbrellas as canes — **"twin dudes."** Hire onto a Sandefjord ship in ballast bound Norway ($30/mo). As their new ship passes their old American ship leaving Bordeaux, **the American captain dips the national colors three times** in farewell to Olavus and the Dane — unprecedented for common sailors. New officers treat them with reverence thereafter.
- Olavus falls off jibguys into sea (rope stuck in tar); fished out from towed boat. Sandefjord captain elderly, black full beard, jovial. Mustered out at Sandefjord after about a month.
- Home via coast steamer → [Kristiansand](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Kristiansand) → Hamburg mail-steamer *Kong Sverre* → Stavanger. Late July 1878.

### Summer 1878 — homecoming to Olava
- *Ch 31 / summertime-in-norway.* Meeting with Olava strange — she has grown, is 19, "no longer the little girl" but a fully developed woman, more beautiful. She invites him home to **Roalsøy** that afternoon.
- **Olava's family at Roalsøy:**
  - Father: retired farmer, then successful fisherman ("notebar"), amassed fortune; owned "nearly all of Roalsøy"; now aging, poor health. Divided his island among three married sons. A fourth son married a Eide Fogn farm-heiress. Eldest daughter married and resides on [Sandøy](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Sand%C3%B8y) near Roalsøy.
  - Mother: from Rossøy near Finnøy; best-friend of Olavus's mother in youth; told Olavus his mother was "very beautiful, and of a quiet, lovely nature."
  - **Gustav**: youngest brother, married, lives nearby, sailor, big man, calls Olava "the child"; teases her about engagement; friendly.
  - Olava: youngest daughter, only child at home; beloved. Mother's garden and orchard on their kept piece of Roalsøy.
- Father had saved 20 crowns/month of Olavus's *Hjemmet* wages + added to it → 400 crowns for navigation school.
- **"Those were the happiest days of my life."**
- Home on Finnøy via steamer *Rogaland* to [Judaberg](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Judaberg) (east side of Finnøy); walks across the island in "the latter part of July" — "like going through a flower garden the whole way."

### Navigation school, Stavanger — Oct 1878 to April 1879
- *Ch 32 / the-navigation-school.* 120 students, 4 teachers, 1 dean **Gabrielson**. Olavus placed in **Prof. Nilson**'s room (~30 students).
- Classmates named: **Theodor Halvorson** (from *Hjemmet*), **Erik Rasin**, **Laland**, **Tonning**, **Hamre**, **Sivert Murer**, **Christian Naaden** from Finnøy.
- Rents a third-story room from landlord **Kindingstad** on **Bredalmendingen** — convenient to the pier where sailboats from the islands land.
- Borrows ("loan-takes") boats nightly to reach Olava at Roalsøy. One near-miss with a fisherman who recognized his boat was missing; Olavus landed at a different spot.
- Skates with Olava at **Mosvannet** and on **Breivannet** inside Stavanger that winter (must have been very cold). Takes her home to Finnøy for one winter trip — carries her on his arm while skating (she has no skates). Sisters fear he'll drop her.
- Bethania Chapel Sunday evenings — **Rev. Lars Oftedal** preaches, **Mr. Davidson** directs choir (a cappella).
- Spring 1879: develops a cough; suspects **tuberculosis** (siblings have died of it at about this age). Consults **Dr. Due** — "Not yet, but it can become tuberculosis." Codliver oil prescribed. Depressed, dispirited.
- **April 1, 1879: passes navigation exam.** Examiner **Capt. Knap** (Navy). Censors: **Capt. C.H. Paderson**, **N. Hillieson**. Six applicants — only Olavus and **Hamre** pass. Olavus helped Hamre cheat (correct answer: "by the latitude scale" for chart-measuring). Hamre thanks him afterward; says his mother would have been disappointed. They part. Diploma in pocket, but sick, money spent, unmarriageable.

### Summer 1879 interlude — fishing trip with Peder
- *Ch 33 / a-fishing-trip.* Youngest brother **Peder** (2½ years younger). Father's boat → Stavanger for herring bait → Olavus's future father-in-law (Olava's father) loans deep-sea lines and five witch-nets → **Vignesholmene** islands SW of Finnøy. Eider-duck eggs (goose-sized) gathered for egg-cakes. Salmon fry. Caught sea bass, blue fish, red fish, cod, deep-sea grayfish, dog/tiger fish, salmon, trout, lobsters, and a seafowl in a witch-net. Sells surplus at Stavanger fishmarket.

### Voyage 6 — schoonerbrig *Allegro* — June 1879, age 19
- **Role:** boatswain and sailmaker (navigator could fill in for mate if needed). **Route:** Stavanger → Vardøy (salt cargo for Russian fishermen).
- Hired via **Helland** again. Captain: small, middle-aged, black full beard, tight-fisted.
- 1st mate: **Abraham Hjelm** (brother of Paul Hjelm, mate on *Nanna*). Straightforward.
- Former captain/part-owner of *Allegro* was known along the coast as **"Fa'en i Nordsjøan"** ("Satan of the Northsea").
- **Crew:**
  - **Frøyland**: seaman, ~25, proud, self-conceited; resents Olavus for taking the best forecastle seat and for outranking him.
  - **Tobias Bjøravaag** from Finnøy: young, large, brave youngman on first voyage.
- Forecastle under main deck — small, dark, dirty, stinking, no ventilation. Clumsy rigging: crew pulls for dear life every tack — "by planned arrangement to keep the crew in slavery."
- Route: up the coast in June, Lofoten, North Cape, Nordkyn/Kinnarodden, Vardøy. "Everything was gray, gray, gray." Laps/Finns with mortarboard-like caps (red-line trim). Russian fishing boats (one mast forward, small aft, axe-cut planks). 24-hour business days. Fresh cod in harbor.
- Stockfish drying-racks ½ mile long. Tidal range 23 feet. Capstan-bar heaving and no copper — ship careened on sand bar to be cleaned and tarred.
- Catches bad cold; doctor at Vardøy confirms tuberculosis possible. Captain refuses discharge unless he can find replacement; eventually finds two who will sail to Hamburg without pay. Captain tries to short Olavus 4 kroner ($1) on pay-off; mustering officer forces payment.
- *Ch 34 / a-fight-with-tuberculosis.* 2100 miles from home, no money, no health. In harbor also lies **steamer *Diana* of Bergen, Capt. Sars**, loading stockfish. Olavus rows out, offers to work passage. Accepted.

### Voyage 7 — steamer *Diana*, Capt. Sars — July-Aug 1879
- *Ch 35 / captain-sars-of-the-diana.* Everything inside painted white; open round windows; unoccupied bed waiting. Christian older man reads Bible daily — his influence keeps crew clean-spoken. Best food Olavus ever had at sea.
- Inside-coast route: Hammerfest → Tromsøy → Rystrømmen strait (current 7 mph, ship speed 7 mph — balance) → Senja, Harstad, Vestfjord → Bodøy → Torghatten (island with hole through it) → Folda weather-delay → narrow sound past Kristiansund → Molde → Aalesund → Nordfjord (steep mountain-wall echoes via steam siren) → **Bergen** after 7 days.
- At Bergen: unloading work (24-hr shifts, 4.50–9 kr/day), loading stockfish for Mediterranean. Captain Sars's fatherly care, board on ship. *Diana* history: collided with Russian Emperor's private yacht in Black Sea, sank it; detained at Odessa with chains and locks; crew cleared by maritime court. Olavus wants to continue with *Diana* to Mediterranean but spot already filled. Leaves early September 1879 with ~200 kroner and cured cough. Coast-steamer to Stavanger.
- **Later news:** *Allegro* **sank near Cuxhaven**. All saved but lost belongings. First of three ships that wreck shortly after Olavus leaves them.

### Voyage 8 — bark *Semiramis*, East-India-man — Oct 1879 to Oct 1880, ages 19–20
- **Role:** common seaman. **Route:** [Flensberg](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Flensburg) → Shields (coal) → Rangoon, Burmah → St. Helena → Ascension → Falmouth (orders) → Copenhagen. One year exactly.
- Hired via **Helland** again; 15 men; owners ordered first-class steamer + rail + best meals all the way to Flensberg.
- **Captain Hans Hamre**: middle-aged, large, brave-looking, "fearfully proud." Former officer in American navy, freemason of high order with regalia, married to owner's daughter. Proud of his East-India-man.
- 1st mate: elderly, former captain reduced for drinking, easy but timid of captain.
- 2nd mate: **John Lund** from [Hundvaag](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Hundv%C3%A5g,_Stavanger), big middle-aged, striving mightily for captain's favor.
- Boatswain: 40+, former mate, reserved, thinks himself a cut above.
- Carpenter: little modest man from Hogganvik or [Etne](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Etne). Captain's favorite, quiet orderly. Sailed with this ship a long time.
- Sailmaker: tall, husky, Stavanger.
- **Old Thompson** (the *Faina* sailor) appears again — now envious Olavus has outranked him; goes silent after Olavus rebukes.
- **Wilhelm Jacobson**: common seaman, ~28, extraordinarily well-built, strong, good-humored, good comrade. City lad.
- **"Thora's Sven"** (illegitimate son of a Stavanger woman named Thora): ~28, small, thin, boastful, pretends to be a great fighter, "licked all the policemen in Stavanger." Old comrade of Jacobson. Calls country boys "bondeknøler." **City vs country tension in forecastle.**
- Unnamed **cabin boy** from [Horten](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Horten) — son of Capt. Hjort of the Norwegian navy. Hale and hearty; swims to the English ship with Olavus.
- **Kristian Tronser** from [Sandnes](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Sandnes): boy, has an accordion; Olavus borrows it and plays hymns. Illiterate when they start — Olavus teaches him navigation on the homeward voyage and he does surprisingly well.
- Steward from [Farsund](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Farsund), middle-aged, big black full beard — the **Neptune-ritual victim** at equator crossing.
- **Captain's wife** aboard to Shields — daughter of the ship-owner, much younger than captain; good-looking and kindly. Orders a sumptuous birthday dinner on the run to England: "Poor boys; they have little else for joy onboard than something good to eat." (*Ch 37 / the-birthday-of-the-captains-wife.*)
- **Jacobson–Sven–Olavus fight:** *Ch 37.* Sven wanted Olavus's forecastle seat at the table to put Jacobson next to him. After a cap-knocked-off-by-accident pretext, **Sven grabs Olavus's throat**; Olavus throws him against wall, pins him, chokes him blue in the face. Jacobson jerks Olavus onto the table; Sven lands on top; Olavus keeps the throat grip from below. Sven gives up. Silent forecastle. Jacobson warns Olavus on deck that Sven "overcame three policemen in one fight"; Olavus tells him to warn Sven off. **After this day, "bondeknøl" is never heard again** and comradeship develops. Sven and Olavus eventually good friends. 1,350 tons of coal loaded at Shields.
- *Ch 38 / off-to-the-tropics:* Flying fish on deck; phosphorescent sea; waterspout/cloudburst at Olavus's helm (sky-to-sea spiral column rises ~5 mi away).
- *Ch 39 / neptune-as-barber:* Equator crossing ritual against the black-bearded steward. Anonymous Neptune with gold crown (moonlit), 30-inch wooden knife, pot of soot-and-grease. Paints steward's beard, shaves with wooden blade. Steward takes it peaceably.
- *Ch 40 / the-flying-dutchman:* Mirage of a phantom bark seen near Cape of Good Hope after 6 weeks of empty horizon — vanishes silently.
- *Ch 41 / rangoon-burmah:* Approach from sea — **golden pagoda appears first** rising from the horizon blue, then green treetops, then forested hills. Awaited fairyland. March 1880, ~16° N, hot. 90 days, 12,000 miles from England.
- *Ch 42 / peaceful-hindoos:* 80 coolies unloading coal. 20-lb baskets, silver rings as banking, 2½p/day, "Dalai Lama" prayers on mats at noon.
- *Ch 43 / the-pagoda:* White masonry lion (14 ft) and covered stairways with beggars, smaller lions. Gilded temple. Small recesses with family groups of copper/bronze gods; gold-leaf stuck to gods as expiation; tower-chimes ("evil spirits kept away") rang at irregular intervals audibly from the ship at night.
- Sickness: English doctor visits daily. Olavus and others spend 4–5 days in bed in rotation; no one gets it twice. Cork helmets.
- Hawks (like gray doves) feed at his feet on deck; "quite good friends, considering they were hawks."
- *Ch 44 / wise-elephants:* Sawmill with two teakwood-carrying elephants. Owner's grass-feeding story. **Norwegian vs British saloon brawl** — Arendal ship crew and *Semiramis* crew vs. British sailors — Norwegians win; some Britishers leap into river. *Semiramis* cook with two holes in head; Sven with bloody nose and torn shirt. "All pigs are black in the dark."
- **The "World Post" letter:** Olavus buys 30×36-inch rice-paper and writes Olava an entire double-sided sheet. Stavanger gossip calls it "Verdensposten" (*Ch 44.*).
- **The diving incident:** Olavus holds breath swimming straight down; reaches colder water, turns up swimming away from ship; surfaces nearly twice as far as the others, nearly exhausted. "No other Norwegian has ever been so near the bottom of the Rangoon river by swimming, and lived to tell."
- **Second diving incident:** Swims with cabin boy Hjort to English ship 1/4 mile away. Slippery slime on timbers — slips, front-slams water, spits blood on return. Minor internal injury.
- 16,000 sacks of rice loaded ~300 lb each. Rice freight = $16,000 net (coal freight paid all ship's costs for the year).
- Departure early May 1880.
- *Ch 45 / now-back-to-europe:* Sumatra strait danger. Captain buys two apes in Rangoon. Wild one escapes to rigging and is thrown overboard one night during a tack. Tame one — the **monkey-and-paint incident** (pours paint pot out across hatch cover, tries to spread with hands, innocent face when officer returns; mate furious but can't touch the captain's pet). Olavus barbers Saturdays (including Sven); plays Kristian's accordion (hymns); teaches navigation to Jacobson, Thompson, Sven, Kristian.
- **Sunday-strike:** crew refuses Sunday-morning scrubbing. Captain suspects Olavus as ringleader (confirms with oblique remarks about "the Finnøybu" and the dip-net pastor). Captain hostile to Olavus thereafter.
- *Ch 46 / a-seamonster:* 15-foot tailfin sighting near Madagascar (Olavus at helm). Unexplained.
- Albatross fishing around Cape of Good Hope. Single successful catch after cape doves — albatross releases hook by rapid head-throwback trick. One kept on deck several hours; 11-ft wingspread; embalmed for taxidermy.
- Captain tries again to force afternoon-free-watch painting of forecastle — crew refuses. "Rebellious leader" rebuke; cigar thrown to deck; nothing further.
- St. Helena (15° S) telegraph hopes — no luck; Ascension (8° S) — "Semiramis, of Stavanger, passed Ascension today. All well." First news home in 4 months.
- **Scurvy**: Jacobson first. Captain orders busy-work to keep crew moving. Apples at Falmouth — Kristian bites, gums tear and bleed. Orders for Copenhagen. Christmas-feast onboard rapidly restores them.
- *Ch 47 / gay-copenhagen* (wrong chapter heading — this is home-port arrival): **2nd mate cornered by 14 crew in alley** — threatens to kill him for homeward-voyage busy-work. Olavus refuses and **stands with the mate; Jacobson joins.** Mate takes the two of them out for extravagant treats; tells them what the captain said: "If it wasn't for Jakobson and Vestbo, he would show the men who was in command of the ship, but those two boys I am not so sure I can handle."
- Skagen stop — captain too proud to shelter with "Chinese junk" fleet of 200 small ships waiting for wind. Tacks for three days. Wind shifts, proud entry to Copenhagen — first of all into harbor. Captain's vanity, Olavus steering "as if the helm was red-hot."
- **Trekroner landing:** captain insists on going ashore from 3 mi out in high sea despite pilot's warning. Olavus, Jacobson, Thompson, carpenter chosen as best hands. Boat rudder floats away; 2nd mate steers with oar; carpenter's boathook breaks. Land wet. Hotel — "sea-legs" wobble. Captain praises them lavishly at hotel to other captains.
- One year exactly on the voyage. 152 days on moving water.

### Voyage 9 — brig *Olivier* of Drammen — ~Oct to Dec 1880
- **Role:** 2nd mate. Route: Copenhagen → Sundsvall, Sweden (lumber) → Leith/Venness, Firth of Forth. **42-year-old ex-French-man-of-war, blunt both ends, terrible sailer.**
- Ship-models in Rangoon sent home via *Semiramis* boys. Olavus determined to go home only as a real styrmann (navigator), not just a diploma-holder.
- *Ch 48 / a-memorable-voyage-in-the-baltic:* Loading square timbers at Sundsvall in Gulf of Bothnia, autumn → early winter. Timbers ice-coated; snow, ice in harbor, tug breaks ice to sea. Ship exterior becomes foot-thick ice from frozen spray — danger of capsize.
- **Olavus's frozen feet:** cavalry boots bought in Copenhagen have inner sole-humps that block circulation; loses use of feet, offered oversized boots by a crewmate saves them.
- Bornholm near-shipwreck; clears foreland by yards. Three days tacking.
- **Falsterbo light trick:** Olavus spots the light-ship windward-bow just in time — two minutes more would have run aground on the sandbar, where three wrecks visible at daylight.
- *Ch 49 / sailing-in-fear-of-shipwreck:* Between Kattegat and Skagen. Captain: "If we are not outside of Skagen light before four o'clock tomorrow morning we shall be shipwrecked." **Olavus at helm 8 hours straight** in snowstorm, topgallants standing through the gale, passing small-sail fleets who stare in disbelief. Lets a mittenless relief go get his mittens; captain then holds Olavus at the wheel. At 3:30 am passes Skagen; storm ceases; captain orders all sails furled; then a **hurricane from NW breaks** — **120 ships wrecked in Kattegat that morning**, "the greatest catastrophe at sea in those waters which had happened in that half century." Olavus's rotten-box brig survives. Captain: old Baltic hand, knew the weather shift was coming.
- *Ch 50 / longing-for-sunny-seas-and-tradewinds:* Nov/Dec 1880. Mustered out at Leith. Captain gives excellent letter of recommendation. Train to Greenock.
- **Later news:** *Olivier* takes coal cargo, springs leak, sinks returning to Leith — condemned, never returns to Norway. **Second ship to wreck after Olavus leaves.**
- *Cito* of Mandal seen in Greenock — 8 days out of Tron with coal, back to Falmouth for repairs.

### Voyage 10 — schoonerbrig *Favorit* of Kristiansand — Dec 1880 to ~March 1881
- **Role:** 2nd mate. **Captain Reinertson.** Route: Greenock → Troon (coal) → [Cienfuegos, Cuba](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Cienfuegos) (sugar) → Boston.
- *Ch 51 / to-cuba-for-a-load-of-sugar.* Severe tornado in Bay of Biscay — ship on side 15–20 seconds, all sails blown to rags flying off like sparrows. Three weeks sewing new sails in tradewinds. Cienfuegos: Mexican-dollar "Florida Water" perfume (made in New York, label); rotting cats and dogs in streets. Shark harpoon battle lost when captain refuses to scratch the gold-plated nameplate.
- 580 hogsheads brown sugar. Olavus and 1st mate eat sugar by the bowlful. Captain: "If I were to furnish you men with sugar then you may be sure you would not get that amount of it."
- Hogsheads break loose north of Bahamas — gas in hold prevents entry. At Boston unloading finds 2–3 feet of loose sugar in bottom of hold, dirty mud with tobacco spittle.
- *Ch 52–53 / in-boston, the-wild-alarm-clock:* Spring 1881. American pilot with silk hat. **Oil-painted enlarged photograph of Olava** made by shipboard painter in a fine frame (metal plate 10×12 inches). Still in Olavus's possession 58 years later — source of late-life tears. **First-alarm-clock episode** — Olavus meddles with captain's unopened package; nearly caught when the bell won't stop. Petroleum cargo for [Karlshamn](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Karlshamn), Sweden.
- *Ch 54 / the-atlantic-ocean-in-fair-weather:* Makes a **second model of *Semiramis*** during easy 30-day crossing Boston → Karlshamn. The first he had already sold to a Norwegian lady in Greenock.
- *Ch 55 / careless-sailors:* 4 am tragicomic near-collision with a Kragerø lumber-bark whose crew were all below drinking coffee. Captain's Norwegian curses at them.
- *Ch 56 / harbors-of-the-baltic:* Karlshamn summer — two friendly policemen as sailor-companions. Tailor makes Olavus his best-ever suit. Captain tries to keep him; Olavus discharges. Unexpectedly excellent recommendation letter. Coast-steamer to Copenhagen.

### Voyage 11 — bark *President Daae* of Tvedestrand — Aug 1881 to Dec 1881
- **Role:** 1st mate at 21 — highest Norwegian mate's pay. **Captain Thoresen.** Route: Stralsund (Germany) → New York → Helsingør (for orders) → Danzig → home to Tvedestrand.
- Hired via a Copenhagen ship-broker. First-class steamer ticket Copenhagen → Malmö.
- **Ch 58 / a-perilous-adventure.** On the steamer, Olavus meets **the Swedish lady** — on promenade deck, asks if it will storm (she gets seasick), straightforward, educated, pretty, black hair/eyelashes, soft voice. From "N.N." in Skåne near Malmö. Father a manufacturer. Week's visit in Copenhagen, now going home. Brother in New York she wants Olavus to visit. **Exchange addresses.** Disappears at Malmö. Olavus writes from Stralsund.
  - **Adaptation note:** The memoir is explicit that Olavus is attracted to her, that the "love affair" is delicate and mutual, and that she is "so entirely different from the women sailors usually meet with." For the trilogy's Book II, this near-betrayal-of-Olava is a major sourced strand; see section 4 below.
- **Stralsund:** Captain Thoresen (~50). 2nd mate: 20-year-old from Tvedestrand, heavy long red full-beard down nearly to waist (looks 35). Carpenter from Lillesand, 25–30. Three Norwegians + German crew. German steward (~30).
- **Steward attack:** steward hadn't cleaned Olavus's room; Olavus asked him civilly; steward struck him over the head with a club from behind. Olavus saw stars; seized steward, danced around deck, pinned him between rail and spar. Afterward **steward becomes utterly loyal** — voluntary slave. Accompanies Olavus to Norway at end; gives photo at parting; remembered fondly.
- Shetland Isles — Olavus admires a snug green fjord on the south side, thinks he could live there. Newfoundland Banks — fished fresh cod during a calm.
- **New York:** arrival outside Brooklyn. Captain Thoresen tells Olavus: "If the crew wants to desert, let them go." All desert except the German steward. **Peder Gjesdal** (Stavanger native, feared NY/Brooklyn Norwegian runner, armed, notorious even to police) — told in Ch 61 with two stories of captains who outwitted him (one shot a bullet through his hat; one ship lashed him to the mast all night).
- **Two letters at NY:** Olava (thanks for ship model + painting, asks for his portrait). **Swedish lady: declares love, encloses her picture, wants to marry him.** Olavus has not told her he is engaged. Writes a "diplomatic letter" saying he is not the right man for her.
- *Ch 62–63 / mates-responsibilities, the-dives-in-brooklyn:* Cordage stolen from forecastle side out of sight — $15 Olavus agrees to repay. Young Stavanger-area man from [Tastad](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Tasta), Jæderen takes him on a "tour" of Brooklyn that ends at three increasingly sketchy establishments (magician dens with exit-tickets, a brothel-saloon where girls grab his new Copenhagen straw hat for ransom, a near-naked woman on a sofa). Olavus ends the evening in disgust.
- *Ch 64 / second-mate-goes-mad:* At midnight 2nd mate and carpenter return drunk and brawling; carpenter bites off 2nd mate's finger. Mate howls and yells, goes delirious, jumps overboard. Olavus pursues alone in a small boat with one oar, wriggling stern-row; mate dives under boat repeatedly, swims yelling "Here you will see a fellow who can swim," in English ("seemed to have forgotten to speak Norwegian"). Olavus dunks him multiple times, returns him to the lighter alongside ship, where captain is kicked in the face tying him up. **Mate sings an opsang as he is hoisted aboard bound.** Three of them bloody rags, laughing despite themselves. Mate goes silent for weeks.
- *Ch 65 / captain-on-vacation:* Captain Thoresen disappears ashore to his New York stevedore brother for a week at a time. Olavus handles every authority and moves ship under Brooklyn Bridge (topgallant masts down) to new anchorage. Takes ship to Hoboken. 4,000 barrels petroleum loaded.
- *Ch 66 / lost-on-manhattan-island:* Olavus lost walking back from Bowery 11pm–3:30am — "streets full of tall dark buildings … not a person to be seen," "this would be an excellent place for robbers to do business."
- *Ch 67 / a-new-york-crew:* Captain hires cheapest crew — 8 Norwegian landcrabs and drunks. Forecourse-furling crisis on the way to Helsingør — 9 men can't fasten the sail. Captain orders Olavus aloft as a mate — Olavus obeys but angry, scolds the men down, lets second mate and carpenter stay; when the jib breaks shortly after, Olavus mends it better than new. Captain never complains of his sail-use after this.
- **Captain's cracked coffee-mug** — always uses the same white-and-blue-striped mug with a crack from top to bottom. Superstition as good-luck charm (revealed later).
- **Matches in cabin:** gas from leaking petroleum; one night a man forgets and strikes match — flash but no explosion.
- **Helsingør, early November 1881:** Captain brings two letters. Mine from Olava: come home at end of this voyage, I have waited long. Mine from Swedish lady: **flaming love letter**, will come to him wherever the ship goes if he writes the next port; wants to marry unconditionally.
- Olavus asks for discharge (time up). Captain begs him to stay — Olavus agrees only if captain never mentions the $15 cordage debt again. Agreed.
- **Decision on the Swedish lady:** sees that any reply will bring her to him. Decides to **cut her off silently** — never write again. "She was like a luminous meteor in the sky of my earthly life." Writes: "Thus I lost my Swedish friend, as quickly and unexpectedly as I had met her." **This is explicit material for Book II.**
- Danzig — sandbar trick (dead course). Not permitted to sleep aboard because of petroleum gas. Broker's clerk spies on the Saturday beer-bribed unload. Beer-pail relay unloads full cargo in one day. Captain receives extra-barrel payment, splits 20 Marks each with Olavus. Extra barrel per his own count correction in NY.
- Out of Danzig with ballast for Norway. Big side-wheeler tug. **The drunken broker's clerk** accompanies captain to say goodbye at sea; seasick and drunk; hoisted in an armchair-swing to the tug; second mate's lead-line too short → man and chair plunge into sea → hauled back aboard Olavus's ship → dunked again to tug → finally untied by Germans with knives. "It was fates playing in the affair."
- Baltic → North Sea → Norwegian coast near Tvedestrand. Northwest gale, can't come in without pilot. **The pilot jumps into the sea** from his tiny half-submerged pilot-boat as it passes *President Daae*, a rope tied round his waist; crew hauls him aboard. His **15–16-year-old son** steers the boat away. Father commands: "Set sail and follow the boy in the boat." Boy leads ship through breakers into safe bay. Anchor down in Tvedestrand home port. Mid-December 1881.
- *Ch 68 / homeward-bound:* Olavus stays aboard overnight with the carpenter and the German steward during a hurricane — puts the cable through the rock-point iron ring single-handed. Captain Thoresen invites him home; sister ~30, pale, pleasant, manages his house (captain is unmarried). Three days of parties, introductions as "the best mate I have had." Captain books coast-steamer passage. Coast-steamer to Stavanger — captain ~30 hrs, 10 kr fare, in his own cabin.
- **Home to Olava.** Two years and three months absent.
- **Three-Sunday banns** required. **Wedding: January 22, 1882.**

### Marriage and the last ship
- Near Christmas 1881, engagement sealed. Jan 22, 1882 wedding. Olavus 22, Olava 22.
- A month after the wedding Olavus hears *President Daae* has wrecked on west coast of Jutland. **"The old coffee-mug, containing the captain's good luck, was not with him on this voyage."** All crew saved; belongings lost.
- **Third ship to wreck after Olavus leaves. "Providence plays its part."**

### Late-life frame
- Memoir finished Payallup, WA (1939). Olavus is the only one left; Olava dead before 1939. Still has the oil-painted portrait; cries looking at it. Signs "O. W. Vestbo" Apr 8 1939 at age 79. (P.S.) written on what would have been Olava's 80th birthday. Memoir is for her.

**Adaptation notes — the Swedish-lady episode:** this is the memoir's only explicit near-betrayal-of-Olava moment (Olavus calls it "one of the most strange love affairs of my life" and "hard for me to think I must forever forego hearing from or seeing her again"; "there is a tender spot in my heart for her"). - keep the Swedish lady and make her Olav's first female dual-loyalty crisis (straight intrusion on the betrothal);

- layer both — a Swedish woman and a Swedish man in parallel or in sequence.

The structural material (meeting on a southbound boat, address exchange, first port letter, second port declaration, secret decision to go silent without explanation) is the memoir's most complete emotional subplot and maps cleanly to a queer reinterpretation.

## 8. Themes and patterns (usable as structural hooks)

- **Providence:** three ships wreck after Olavus leaves; miraculous rain at mid-Atlantic; the coffee-mug talisman; his prayers at the anchor windlass. Olavus sees this plainly.
- **City boy / country boy:** *Semiramis* Sven uses "bondeknøl" as a slur; Olavus rebukes by fight; resolves into genuine friendship. Useful for trilogy class dynamics — Jens Hestby is inland-farm stock; Olav is coastal; others are city.
- **Name and identity:** "Finnøybu" (from Capt G.K.'s 1876 naming) becomes Olavus's own self-understanding; "bjørn" (from 1st mate on *Hjemmet*, after learning his surname was Bjørnson) he resents as provocation and accepts as a fate; "Willie" (from Mary Ellen and Margit) is tender and private; "Bill" (on the US bark) is anonymous.
- **Opsang (chorus-song) at departure** — group-voice ritual; compare with the opsang sung by the mad second mate while bound and hoisted in Ch 64.
- **Gate-reading captains:** Capt G.K. (tyrant), Capt Tollefson (saint), Capt Sars (father), Capt Hamre (proud), Capt Thoresen (mercurial bribe-er). Each ship is a separate domestic social world, and Olavus tests each one the way he tests men in forecastle fights.
- **The Swedish lady as Book II mirror:** silent cutoff "before the bubble bursts."
- **Marriage at 22 to his 18-year-old horizon's answer.** Olava's "yes" arrives from West Africa. This is not just domestic but mythic in how Olavus frames it.
- **Tuberculosis as a shadow:** siblings dead of it; Dr. Due confirms "not yet but it may come"; Vardøy doctor confirms; the sea cures him ("tired of going around and waiting for death"); 58 years later an X-ray shows the scar. The memoir's most direct statement of how a sailor's life saved him. For the trilogy where the protagonist already lives in a queer-awakening frame, TB becomes an overlay: the sickness and the desire he can't name both threatening the same body.
- **No queer content in the memoir.** Close male friendships are named (Bertel carpenter; boatswain on *Faina*; Thomas from Stjernerøy; Jacobson; the Danish one-eyed sailor; the German steward turned voluntary slave after the beating). But nothing named in a queer register. The trilogy's queer work is entirely additive — the memoir gives no support or obstacle beyond normal 19th-century sailor mores. The **German steward** is perhaps the most resonant unused figure: a grown man who attacks Olavus with a club from behind, is pinned in a dance around the deck, then "became a voluntary slave to me. He was ever on the go to see what could be done to please me … touching to see how concerned he was for my welfare … gave me a picture of himself when we parted, with a wish I would remember him in kindness; and that I have done to this day." Perfect structural clay for a queer reinterpretation.
