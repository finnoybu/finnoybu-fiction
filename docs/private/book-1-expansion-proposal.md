# Book I Expansion Proposal — Reviewer Recommendation

**From:** Tier-1 reviewer session
**Date:** 2026-04-21
**Status:** Recommendation, awaiting forward-writing-session execution

---

## Baseline

- Current Book I: **76,679 words** across 30 chapters + introduction (~295 pages at 6×9 / 260 wpp)
- Books II/III: ~85-100K words each (~330-385 pages)
- Trade fiction commercial range: 80-150K words
- **Recommended target: ~103K words (+26K, ~395 pages).** Brings Book I into structural parity with its siblings and into the trade-fiction sweet spot for queer historical romance.

## Approach

Add **9 new chapters** at 2,500-3,500 words each, organized in three tiers by architectural priority. Tier A chapters fill scenes the trilogy actively references but never depicts. Tier B chapters give underdeveloped characters depth. Tier C chapters extend existing arcs.

Recommend Tier A first (3 chapters, ~9.5K words), then Tier B #6 (Captain Lindøy, ~3K), then the rest as bandwidth allows.

---

## Tier A — gaps the trilogy actively requires

### 1. **"Christmas Eve, 1879" — the conversation in Olava's bedroom**

**Position:** Insert between current Ch 21 *The Bed in Her Parents' House* and Ch 22 *The Cough Returns*. New numbering shifts later chapters by one.

**Word target:** ~3,500 words.

**Why:** This is the trilogy's most-cited foundational scene. Books II and III reference it ten-plus times as the moment Olava named the form of the marriage — the not-asking, the agreement that Olav would bring her what she needed to know when the carrying got too heavy. It's currently off-page. The trilogy's central marriage-architecture moment shouldn't be off-page.

**Structural anchors (must honor):**
- Olava is 19 (turns 20 next spring per Ch 21).
- Olav is 21 (per Ch 21 reference in current text).
- Setting: Olava's bedroom at the Lindøy house. Her father is in the back parlour across the hall (per Book III Ch 27 retrospective).
- The captain has decided not to be in the kitchen at the time the conversation happens (per Book III Ch 27 retrospective).
- Olava produces these specific phrases (per Book III Ch 23 ledger entry): *"I have told him I know there is a piece. I have told him I will not ask. I have told him I agree to carry the not-asking with him. I have told him that at some future moment in our marriage, if the carrying becomes too heavy, I will ask — and that at that moment he will bring me whatever I need to know. Not before. He has agreed."*
- This is not yet an engagement; the engagement signing is a year later, November 1880.
- Olava records the conversation in the back of her engagement-year notebook (per Book III Ch 23).

**Key beats:**
- The walk up from the kitchen to the bedroom, alone for the first time
- The careful Norwegian-girl-of-19 register Olava uses to name what she has been observing in Olav since they met at Landa six months earlier
- Olav's surprise / careful response — at 21 he has not yet had the language for what she is naming
- The "agree to carry" phrasing
- The "if the carrying becomes too heavy" escape clause
- Closing: small physical gesture (her hand on his face? her forehead against his?) that establishes the pre-engagement physical register
- Olav going back across the hall to the parlour where the captain is reading
- The captain's not-asking (small confirmation he understands what just happened)

### 2. **"The Empty Months" — Olava POV, autumn 1880**

**Position:** Insert between current Ch 25 *Certificate* and Ch 26 *The Silent Engagement*. New chapter would be set August-October 1880.

**Word target:** ~3,000 words.

**Why:** Book I has zero Olava-POV chapters. Book II has two (Chs 9, 20). Book III has three (Chs 6, 14, 23). Adding one Olava-POV chapter to Book I gives the trilogy's two-voice architecture a foundation in Book I rather than introducing it suddenly in Book II. The autumn-1880 Olava-waiting-for-the-engagement period is the natural slot — Olav has his certificate, he's writing letters to ship companies, he has no berth, the engagement signing is months away.

**Structural anchors:**
- Olava is 20 (turning 21 in spring 1881).
- The engagement is not yet signed but is understood by both households as forthcoming.
- Marit is 17 and engaged to the schoolmaster's son.
- The captain's wife has begun the cross-channel mother-correspondence with Mrs. Hestby that becomes the trilogy's mother-network spine.

**Key beats:**
- Olava's small daily life at Lindøy through the autumn
- The first weekly letter exchange beginning (Wed/Sun rhythm later established)
- Marit's small teasing about the engagement
- A scene at the back-garden bench alone where Olava registers what she has decided about the marriage's shape
- Captain Lindøy in the boat-shed visible from the kitchen window (sets up Tier B #6)
- The first ledger entry — Olava buying the small cheap octavo notebook at Stavanger bookbinder in November 1880 (per Book III Ch 23)

### 3. **"Mrs. Hovland's, December 1876" — Wilmington Christmas with Thomas**

**Position:** Insert between current Ch 11 *Vaageland* and Ch 12 *A Cough*. New chapter would be set December 1876.

**Word target:** ~3,000 words.

**Why:** Book III Ch 29's graveside catalogue claims Thomas Engelstad was Olav's "first interior name" — the name he carried for sixty-two years. Book I Ch 11 plants this with ~200 words but the plant is thin. A full chapter showing Olav and Thomas through Christmas 1876 at Mrs. Hovland's, deepening the relationship before Thomas's March 1877 disappearance, would land the Book III claim properly. The drawer architecture and the trilogy's first interior name need this depth.

**Structural anchors:**
- Olav is 18 (turns 19 in March 1877).
- Thomas is 23.
- Mrs. Hovland is the Norwegian-Wilmington widow who has been doing this work since 1851.
- Olav has not yet shipped out on the *Anna Maria* (that comes in Ch 12, mid-October by current chronology).
- Wait — actually per Ch 11 the *Anna Maria* takes Olav out on the sixteenth day. So Olav has already shipped out by mid-November '76. The proper window for this chapter is Olav's December 1876 - February 1877 visits back through Mrs. Hovland's between *Anna Maria* coastal runs OR a flashback chapter set in the lodging-house dawn before Olav leaves.
- Per Book III Ch 29 catalogue: Thomas disappeared "the third Friday of March 1877."
- Per Book III commit 50dd6b0: Thomas stayed at Mrs. Hovland's through the winter after Olav shipped out; disappeared March 1877 while Olav was on the *Champion* in Halifax.

**Key beats:**
- Either: (a) a Christmas evening Olav has at Mrs. Hovland's between *Anna Maria* coastal runs, with Thomas; or (b) a prologue-style chapter set in the first two weeks at the lodging-house, dramatizing the daily walks Thomas took with Olav teaching him to walk un-sailorlike
- A small specific moment that establishes Thomas as the "first name" Olav put aside in his interior — without violating the trilogy's restraint (no consummation; the moment is interior recognition, not action)
- Mrs. Hovland watching them with the small not-asking discipline she has been doing for 25 years
- A single physical beat (Thomas's hand briefly on Olav's shoulder at a corner; Olav's not-flinching) that the body remembers
- Closing: a sentence the body remembers when Olav reads the eight-line letter at Mrs. Tjaere's in April 1877

## Tier B — depth for under-developed characters/arcs

### 4. **"Before the Cast-Off" — Olav at seventeen, spring 1876**

**Position:** Insert between current introduction.md and Ch 1 *Cast Off*.

**Word target:** ~2,500 words.

**Why:** Ch 1 opens with Olav at 18 already at the Stavanger quay. The eighteen-year-old's emotional position — innocent, willing, the body that doesn't yet know what's coming — would be deepened by a chapter set 6-8 weeks earlier showing the offer arriving and the family conversation about taking it.

**Structural anchors:**
- Olav is 17 turning 18 in March 1876.
- The *Sigrid* offer arrives at the Hestby kitchen via the postman.
- His father (who at 24 in '57 had walked off the *Aase* at Newcastle) has feelings about the offer.
- His mother has a different set.
- The brother Per has been dead since November 1869 (per Ch 12: Olav was 11).

**Key beats:**
- The kitchen on the day the offer letter arrives
- Jens reading the letter twice
- The small kitchen conversation about the wage, the term, the captain
- Olav at the boat-shed with his father in the days following, working a repair job
- The decision-moment that is not really a decision because it has already been decided
- A small scene at the church wall where Olav stops on a Sunday and looks at his great-grandfather's stone (sets up Ch 28's drawer-letter inheritance)

### 5. **"The Mothers" — Mrs. Hestby and Mrs. Lindøy correspondence, 1880**

**Position:** Insert between current Ch 26 *The Silent Engagement* and Ch 27 *Two Letters*. (After Tier A #2 insertion, this would be a different position number.)

**Word target:** ~2,500 words.

**Why:** The trilogy's mother-network is one of its quiet structural achievements (Mrs. Lindøy / Mrs. Hestby / Mrs. Thoresen / Mrs. Asbjørnsen / Mrs. Tjaere / Mrs. Hovland / Mrs. Pettersen — the women carrying the marriage architecture). It's referenced in passing across all three books but never depicted. A short chapter following the cross-channel correspondence between the two mothers in the engagement period would establish the network as a depicted thing.

**Structural anchors:**
- Mrs. Hestby and Mrs. Lindøy have known each other since the Skjelvik strawberries summer of 1849 (per Book I Ch 18).
- They are arranging the engagement's geography (who travels which crossing, which household hosts which holiday, etc.).
- The correspondence is fortnightly.

**Key beats:**
- A scene at Mrs. Hestby's kitchen reading a letter from Mrs. Lindøy
- A scene at Mrs. Lindøy's kitchen writing a reply
- One small specific arrangement being negotiated (Christmas '80 location? Easter '81 hosting?)
- A sentence one mother writes to the other that names what neither has said aloud about what their children are building
- Could be split-perspective: half the chapter at one kitchen, half at the other

### 6. **"Captain Lindøy at the Boat-Shed" — autumn 1880**

**Position:** Insert between current Ch 26 *The Silent Engagement* and Ch 27 *Two Letters*, OR pair with Tier B #5 above as a companion.

**Word target:** ~3,000 words.

**Why:** Captain Lindøy is currently somewhat schematic. He gets one major scene (Ch 18 boat-shed conversation with Olav at age 20, his "I lost this at Brest in fifty-seven" speech) but his interior is barely depicted. Given that he's the trilogy's longest-lived character (dies 1917 at 99 per Book III Ch 26 fix), and given that his consular intervention on Olav's behalf in Ch 26 is one of the trilogy's most generous male-male protective acts, he deserves a chapter that's his own.

**Structural anchors:**
- Captain Lindøy is 62 in autumn 1880 (born 1818).
- He lost his hand at Brest in 1857 (age 39).
- His son Asbjørn died of the cough in 1875 (age unknown).
- He is preparing to sail to Finnøy to sign the engagement papers in November 1880.

**Key beats:**
- The captain alone in his Lindøy boat-shed in the late autumn evening with the wooden hand
- Polishing the small leather wallet he will carry the engagement papers in (per Book III Ch 26 retrospective)
- His memory of Brest — the rope that took the hand, the French surgeon
- His memory of his son Asbjørn — the cough year
- His evaluation of Olav as the man who will marry his daughter
- The decision to inquire at the consulate about Olav's missing year (sets up Ch 26's "I know about Wilmington" disclosure)
- A small physical beat — he sets the wallet down, he picks it up, he stands at the boat-shed door looking across the channel at Finnøy

## Tier C — extensions of existing arcs

### 7. **"The Diana, Bergen-bound" — second *Diana* chapter**

**Position:** Insert between current Ch 14 *The White Ship* and Ch 15 *Cured*.

**Word target:** ~2,500 words.

**Why:** The *Diana* arc is currently one chapter. Sars is one of the trilogy's three foundational captain-figures (Gjermund, Sars, Brun — later Thoresen builds on this template). A second *Diana* chapter showing one of the eleven days at sea more fully, with another Sars conversation, would deepen Sars and the trilogy's captain-architecture.

**Structural anchors:**
- The *Diana* voyage is summer 1878. Olav is 20.
- The voyage takes 12 days from Vardøy to Bergen.
- Sars's mate is Holm — interesting parallel: Book III's bosun on the *President Daae* is also a Holm. Either same family or coincidence; could explore.

**Key beats:**
- A specific day at sea — perhaps the day the *Diana* passes Trondheim at dawn
- Sars at the rail with another small piece of advice (different from the "next-three-things" speech)
- A glimpse of how the steady eating/sleeping/working is rebuilding Olav's body
- Olav's first noticing he can climb a stair without stopping — physical recovery rendered specifically

### 8. **"Halifax Winter" — the Champion year**

**Position:** Insert between current Ch 11 *Vaageland* and Ch 12 *A Cough*. (After Tier A #3 insertion, this would shift further.)

**Word target:** ~3,000 words.

**Why:** The Halifax winter where the cough first returns is summarized in Ch 11 in two paragraphs. It's the moment Olav realizes the cough hasn't gone — and the moment Captain Snyder pays him off "at the next port." This is dramatically rich and currently underweight.

**Structural anchors:**
- Olav is on the *Champion,* Boston-Halifax run, winter 1876-1877.
- Captain Snyder doesn't employ men he has to feed when they can't work.
- Mrs. Tjaere's at Boston is the lodging-house Olav is ultimately paid off to (per Ch 11).
- Mrs. Hovland's letter about Thomas's disappearance reaches him at Mrs. Tjaere's in early April.

**Key beats:**
- The Halifax cold — render the specific Norwegian-American winter
- The cough returning at a specific moment (a particular morning, a particular sleeve)
- Snyder's small business-like dismissal
- The five days at Mrs. Tjaere's
- The eight-line letter from Mrs. Hovland (currently shown at the end of Ch 11; could be moved here for proximity to the disappearance)
- The decision to find the *Augusta* and go to Quebec

## Constraints — must not contradict

When the forward-writing session executes, the following established trilogy facts must be honored:

- **Olav birth:** August 1858 (per Book III Ch 1 banns: "twenty-four years and seven months" on March 14, 1883). Note: Book II Ch 30 currently has "six days after my birthday" on March 13, 1883 — this is a known flag awaiting fix; do not propagate the March birthday into new Book I chapters.
- **Olava birth:** August 1860 (per Book III Ch 1 banns: "twenty-two years and seven months" on March 14, 1883).
- **Mrs. Hestby:** born 1823, age 60 in 1883 (per Book III post-fix Chs 4, 6).
- **Captain Lindøy:** born 1818, "sixty-fifth year" at March 1883 wedding (per Book III Ch 1).
- **Captain Lindøy lost hand at Brest, 1857.**
- **Per Hestby (Olav's older brother) died of TB November 1869, age unknown** — Olav was 11 (per Ch 12).
- **Asbjørn (Olava's brother) died of TB 1875.**
- **The *Sigrid* of '76: Captain Gjermund. The *Bergljot* of '74 was Thoresen's bark, different ship.**
- **Mathis the *Sigrid* galley-boy died of fever at New Orleans 1877** (per Book III Ch 24 catalogue).
- **Thomas Engelstad disappeared third Friday of March 1877 from Mrs. Hovland's, while Olav was on the *Champion* at Halifax** (per Book III commit 50dd6b0).
- **Anders Lindqvist:** ~28 in September 1882, born ~1854, died on the *Astrid* October 1897 at ~43 (per Book III post-fix Ch 22).
- **Haakon Berg:** 25 in summer 1876, died 2 March 1911 at 60 (per Book III Ch 25).
- **Sven Haugen:** 24 in summer 1881 voyage, born ~1857, wife Hanne, son Jonas b. ~1878, lives at Sandviken in Bergen.
