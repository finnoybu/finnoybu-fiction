# Act III plan — Ch 19-25

> **Scope:** late May 1876 → late July 1876. Ch 19 opens at Vestbø in the home-interval after the *Asta* customhouse confrontation; Act III closes with Olav signing onto the *Dronningen* (memoir *Dronningen*) and the ship taking the wind for Archangel. Act IV opens aboard.
> **Status:** locked 2026-04-25 after Acts I and II reviewed-and-green close. Plan-of-record for Olava's introduction and Karsten activation.
> **Authority:** supplements [trilogy-outline.md](trilogy-outline.md) Book I Act III (which scoped 4 chapters Ch 19-22). This plan widens to **5 firm chapters + 1 reserve** based on density of Olava-introduction + Karsten-activation work.

## Names — locked

- **Olava's family (locked Ken 2026-04-25):**
  - Father: **Bjørn Olsen Lindøy** — aging former fisherman of Lindøy.
  - Mother: **Bertha Nilsdatter Rossøy** — warm matriarch, friend of Olav's mother in youth.
  - Brother: **Gustav** — the teasing sailor, unmarried, on stage in Ch 22 family scenes.
  - Brother: **Bernhard Lindøy** — married, on a divided piece of Lindøy.
  - Brother: **Leonard Lindøy** — married, on a divided piece of Lindøy.
  - Brother: **Vilhelm Lindøy** — married, on a divided piece of Lindøy.
  - **Olava's dead brother (unnamed)** — pre-1876 death, the one she nursed; source of her Ch 21 quiet knowledge of what survival costs. May be named later if a chapter requires it.
- **Karsten Tjørn** — Finnøy farmer on the upper ground above Østhusvik, age 24 in summer 1876, unmarried. Hair grown over the collar (Ch 4 detail). Sits with his mother in the pew across the aisle from the Hestby family at Hesby church. **Object of two prior queer-seed beats: Ch 4 (Hesby winter service) and Ch 7 (pre-departure Sunday — drafted, pending commit per memory).** Father Knut Tjørn (named once Ch 4); brother (Olav's school year, unnamed).
- **Lars Oftedal** — historical figure (1838–1900), Stavanger pietist preacher, Bethania Hall. Used as memoir-sourced backdrop, not a fully rendered character.
- **Cousin John Stensøy** — owner-broker who hired Olav onto *Asta* and heard the customhouse accusation. Likely on stage at the *Dronningen* sign-on (Ch 25) for continuity. Also brief presence at the Ch 23 family dinner at Rossøygate (delivers Jens, has a glass of port at the door, leaves).
- **Captain Tollefson** — *Dronningen* captain (memoir-real, kept). The good-captain counterweight to Gjermund. Brief on-stage at Ch 25 sign-on.
- **The *Dronningen*** ("the Queen") — locked Ken 2026-04-25 as the fictional name for memoir's *Hjemmet*. Replaces earlier provisional *Hjemkomst*. Bark; Captain Tollefson; bound for Archangel late July 1876. Pairs against *Asta* (love) under Gjermund (tyrant) as the queen-ship under the kind captain.
- **Landa and Roda** — kept as memoir-real Finnøy gaard names. Per the names-index naming policy: place names stay; ships rename. Olava and her companion stay at Roda for the visit; Olav and Peder pass Roda on the road back to Vestbø.
- **Stjernarøy spelling** — resolved 2026-04-26 against NRK ordbok. Modern correct form is **Sjernarøy** (no T); previous-variant **Stjernarøy** (with T) is the documented historical form and the period-correct 1875 spelling. Memoir's **Stjernerøy** is plausibly a personal/handwriting variant; trilogy regularizes to Stjernarøy for period-authenticity. Memoir-digest and trilogy-outline source-rows retain memoir's Stjernerøy as citation; trilogy prose, names-index lock, and act plans use Stjernarøy.
- **"youngman"** — established one-word translator-idiom across Ch 6, 8, 9, 14, 17, 18, 20. Period nautical compound (per `reference_youngman_period_term.md`). Not a typo.

## Voice-register — the tripartite split (locked Ken 2026-04-25)

The act's signature technical move. Three distinct sub-registers within Olav-POV chapters:

| Mode | Where | Description |
|---|---|---|
| **Domestic-austere** | Ch 19 Vestbø; Ch 25 quay-departure | Pulled-back to Book I baseline. Scandinavian-plain, object-encoded, refuses interior. Returns the prose to the trilogy's origin to mark the homecoming as restoration. Same register as Ch 1, Ch 4, Ch 7. |
| **Olava-warmth** | Ch 20 field; Ch 22 Bredalmendingen; Ch 23 yellow-ribbon dinner; Ch 25 goodbye | Loosens around Olava's presence in Olav's air. Not Miller-adjacent yet — too early in Book I — but the prose admits more weather, more color, more specific physical attention than the domestic mode permits. Earned to a person, not to a time-position on the gradient. |
| **Third mode (around male interest)** | Ch 24 Bethania Hall (Karsten on stage) | A different warmth from the Olava-warmth. Specifically: the body-knowing-where-someone-is-in-the-room mode. Compressed sentences. Object-noting at higher specificity (the back of the neck, the hand on a hymnal, hair over a collar). The not-speaking as held content. Distinct from the Haakon-POV register because it is rendered from inside Olav's POV; Haakon-POV is rendered from outside Olav. |

Olava-POV (Ch 21) is its own register entirely — Tóibín — and stands outside this tripartite. Cannot accidentally bleed into any of the three Olav-POV modes.

## Open Act-level concerns from prior reviewer reads (carried forward)

From ChatGPT (post-Act-II) and Claude.ai (post-Act-II):

1. **Consequence, not escalation.** Captain degradation closed at Ch 18. Act III's job is to show what Olav does with the man he has become. **Lands as character continuity** — the Customhouse Olav is the Landa Olav. Both Stensøy and Olava treat him as someone whose word counts. *Connective tissue across the act-boundary.*
2. **Unpredictability under known system.** Olava IS the unpredictability ChatGPT asked for. **Lands as Olava's interior** (Ch 21 Tóibín), not as plot-event. The kind-of-attention she offers is the unpredictability — direct, rational, asking-after-fear.
3. **Tighter queer-arc adjacency** (Claude.ai). **Lands as sequence** across Ch 19 → Ch 20 → Ch 21 → Ch 24 — Karsten retrieved before Olava arrives, Olava-arrival uncompressed, Karsten on stage after Olava is committed. Adjacency without simultaneity. Reader carries both knowledges; Olav carries both; neither collapses the other.
4. **Pattern flags from Act II final read.** Short declarative chapter openings — break pattern in Ch 19, 20, 22 (vary opening shape). "He did not..." constructions — keep but trim. Object-encoding density — already at optimal density; do not increase. **Female voice landing** — Ch 21 Olava-POV resolves the Act II concern.

## Act III concerns specific to this act

1. **Olava as person, not foil.** The trilogy's hidden spine has a structural risk if Olava is rendered primarily as the woman-Olav-is-also-loving — she becomes shadow-of-the-real-thread. **Mitigation:** Ch 21 Olava-POV gives her a full chapter at her first appearance. Tóibín register protects her interiority. Her question to Olav is asked because she wants to know, not because the trilogy needs the moment to advance.
2. **Karsten as Haakon-substitute.** Karsten's on-stage moment at Bethania risks reading as "another Haakon." **Mitigation:** Karsten is *not* another Haakon — different register-mode (third-mode vs Proulx), different relationship (no exchange of any kind, ever; Haakon and Olav exchanged work, words, the figure-eight coil), different setting (revival hall vs forecastle). The two queer-presences are not interchangeable; they are two different fronts of the same impossibility.
3. ~~**Stavanger compression.**~~ **Resolved 2026-04-26 by split.** Ch 22 (Stavanger) split into Ch 22 *Bredalmendingen* (Thursday-Friday: boardinghouse, Bjørn, photograph studio) and Ch 23 *The Yellow Ribbon* (Saturday: dinner + card-exchange + Bethania arrangement). The yellow-ribbon recovery of Olav's mother needed proper breathing room and got it as the new Ch 23's emotional center.
4. **Bethania as a religious chapter without a thesis.** Lars Oftedal preaching is real; the prose must not editorialize on pietism, the queer-arc-vs-religion tension, or Karsten-as-Christian-conflicted. **Discipline:** stay in Olav-POV; render the room; let the not-speaking carry the meaning.

## Old-prose conceptual harvest

Old prose covers some of this stretch — the Olava-meeting at Landa exists in pre-restart drafts. **Consult sparingly.** The old prose was written before the tripartite register-split was theorized; old material will lean toward generic-romance register. Concepts to consider:

- **Light blue dress, yellow straw hat, tag game, runs over and grabs his arm** — sourced from memoir, not from old prose. Use memoir wording as the seed.
- **Bethania Hall as a real place in 1876 Stavanger** — research scope: confirm Lars Oftedal preaching schedule for the relevant week if available; otherwise treat as ambient backdrop.

**Style pitfalls:**
- Do not write Olava as the modest-bride archetype. The memoir explicitly flags her direct gesture (running across, grabbing his arm). Tóibín register on her side renders her as a person who decided to do that.
- Do not write the photograph studio as a romance set-piece. The memoir-fact is small; the chapter-weight is the holding-it-after, not the sitting-for-it.
- Do not write Karsten with eye-contact at Bethania. The not-speaking includes not-looking-at-each-other-directly. Olav knows where he is in the room; Karsten knows Olav knows. The body-knowing is the rendered content; the exchange is structural absence.
- Do not let Olava's question to Olav become a thesis-statement scene. Tóibín register: she asks because she wants to know; he cannot answer; the chapter passes.

## Act-IV bridge note

Act III closes as *Dronningen* turns out of Stavanger. **Act IV (Ch 26+) opens aboard.** The Bristol encounter with Haakon (per [project_haakon_bristol_encounter.md]) lives in Act IV; Act III does not foreshadow it.
