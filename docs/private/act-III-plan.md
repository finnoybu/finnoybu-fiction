## Voice-register — the tripartite split (locked Ken 2026-04-25)

The act's signature technical move. Three distinct sub-registers within Olav-POV chapters:

| Mode | Where | Description |
|---|---|---|
| **Domestic-austere** | Ch 19 Vestbø; Ch 24 quay-departure | Pulled-back to Book I baseline. Scandinavian-plain, object-encoded, refuses interior. Returns the prose to the trilogy's origin to mark the homecoming as restoration. Same register as Ch 1, Ch 4, Ch 7. |
| **Olava-warmth** | Ch 20 field; Ch 22 Stavanger courtship; Ch 24 goodbye | Loosens around Olava's presence in Olav's air. Not Miller-adjacent yet — too early in Book I — but the prose admits more weather, more color, more specific physical attention than the domestic mode permits. Earned to a person, not to a time-position on the gradient. |
| **Third mode (around male interest)** | Ch 23 Bethania Hall (Karsten on stage) | A different warmth from the Olava-warmth. Specifically: the body-knowing-where-someone-is-in-the-room mode. Compressed sentences. Object-noting at higher specificity (the back of the neck, the hand on a hymnal, hair over a collar). The not-speaking as held content. Distinct from the Haakon-POV register because it is rendered from inside Olav's POV; Haakon-POV is rendered from outside Olav. |

Olava-POV (Ch 21) is its own register entirely — Tóibín — and stands outside this tripartite. Cannot accidentally bleed into any of the three Olav-POV modes.

## Open Act-level concerns from prior reviewer reads (carried forward)

From ChatGPT (post-Act-II) and Claude.ai (post-Act-II):

1. **Consequence, not escalation.** Captain degradation closed at Ch 18. Act III's job is to show what Olav does with the man he has become. **Lands as character continuity** — the Customhouse Olav is the Landa Olav. Both Stensøy and Olava treat him as someone whose word counts. *Connective tissue across the act-boundary.*
2. **Unpredictability under known system.** Olava IS the unpredictability ChatGPT asked for. **Lands as Olava's interior** (Ch 21 Tóibín), not as plot-event. The kind-of-attention she offers is the unpredictability — direct, rational, asking-after-fear.
3. **Tighter queer-arc adjacency** (Claude.ai). **Lands as sequence** across Ch 19 → Ch 20 → Ch 21 → Ch 23 — Karsten retrieved before Olava arrives, Olava-arrival uncompressed, Karsten on stage after Olava is committed. Adjacency without simultaneity. Reader carries both knowledges; Olav carries both; neither collapses the other.
4. **Pattern flags from Act II final read.** Short declarative chapter openings — break pattern in Ch 19, 20, 22 (vary opening shape). "He did not..." constructions — keep but trim. Object-encoding density — already at optimal density; do not increase. **Female voice landing** — Ch 21 Olava-POV resolves the Act II concern.

## Act III concerns specific to this act

1. **Olava as person, not foil.** The trilogy's hidden spine has a structural risk if Olava is rendered primarily as the woman-Olav-is-also-loving — she becomes shadow-of-the-real-thread. **Mitigation:** Ch 21 Olava-POV gives her a full chapter at her first appearance. Tóibín register protects her interiority. Her question to Olav is asked because she wants to know, not because the trilogy needs the moment to advance.
2. **Karsten as Haakon-substitute.** Karsten's on-stage moment at Bethania risks reading as "another Haakon." **Mitigation:** Karsten is *not* another Haakon — different register-mode (third-mode vs Proulx), different relationship (no exchange of any kind, ever; Haakon and Olav exchanged work, words, the figure-eight coil), different setting (revival hall vs forecastle). The two queer-presences are not interchangeable; they are two different fronts of the same impossibility.
3. **Stavanger compression.** Ch 22 carries a lot — photograph exchange + families meeting + walking on Bredalmendingen. **Mitigation:** if at draft Ch 22 wants to split into 22A (photograph interior) and 22B (families meeting), the act flexes. Reserve slot Ch 24 may absorb instead.
4. **Bethania as a religious chapter without a thesis.** Lars Oftedal preaching is real; the prose must not editorialize on pietism, the queer-arc-vs-religion tension, or Karsten-as-Christian-conflicted. **Discipline:** stay in Olav-POV; render the room; let the not-speaking carry the meaning.

## Old-prose conceptual harvest

Old prose covers some of this stretch — the Olava-meeting at Landa exists in pre-restart drafts. **Consult sparingly.** The old prose was written before the tripartite register-split was theorized; old material will lean toward generic-romance register. Concepts to consider:

- **Light blue dress, yellow straw hat, tag game, runs over and grabs his arm** — sourced from memoir, not from old prose. Use memoir wording as the seed.
- **Bethania Hall as a real place in 1876 Stavanger** — research scope: confirm Lars Oftedal preaching schedule for the relevant week if available; otherwise treat as ambient backdrop.

**Style pitfalls:**
- Do not write Olava as the modest-bride archetype. The memoir explicitly flags her direct gesture (running across, grabbing his arm). Tóibín register on her side renders her as a person who decided to do that.
- Do not write the photograph studio as a romance set-piece. The memoir-fact is small; the chapter-weight is the holding-it-after, not the sitting-for-it.
- Do not write Karsten with eye-contact at Bethania. The not-speaking includes not-looking-at-each-other-directly. Olav knows where he is in the room; Karsten knows Olav knows. The body-knowing is the rendered content; the exchange is structural absence.
- Do not let Olava's question to Olav become a thesis-statement scene. Tóibín register: she asks because she wants to know; he cannot answer; the chapter passes.
