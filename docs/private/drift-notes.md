# Voice-Drift Self-Review Notes

Maintained by the Book-I-expansion session. After each new or expanded chapter is committed, this session reviews the prose against the established voice (Chs 1–3, 6–7, 9, 11, 13–17, 20–29 of Book I) and flags anything that reads as drift — sentence rhythm, idiom, image vocabulary, paragraph closure shapes, dialogue cadence.

The forward-writing session (which wrote the established voice) does the drift-correction pass after Book I expansion is complete.

**Format per entry:**

```
## [Chapter] — committed [hash]

### What I think landed
- Specific things that match voice

### What I flag as possible drift
- file:line — what I see drifting — possible correction (or "no fix from inside")
```

## Reference voice anchors

For self-comparison while reviewing my own drafts, the cleanest established-voice exemplars are:

- **Sentence rhythm at peak emotion:** [03-hogganvik.md:155-159](../../content/en/03-hogganvik.md#L155-L159) — "He thought: *I will learn to put my words in drawers too. That is what the men here have learned. That is how they live.*"
- **Sensory precision (smell/weight/temperature):** [01-cast-off.md:19](../../content/en/01-cast-off.md#L19) — "the standing rigging had been newly tarred; he could smell it over the weed and the salt, a hot dark honeyed smell that would sit in his clothes for the rest of the summer."
- **Wry self-deprecation as armor:** [02-the-captains-smile.md:113](../../content/en/02-the-captains-smile.md#L113) — "He did not tell his stomach what he had done; his stomach found out without him."
- **Desire shown through detail not labeled:** [01-cast-off.md:159](../../content/en/01-cast-off.md#L159) — "the cold rushed into the place where the hand had been, and Olav understood that he had been warm there without noticing"
- **Paragraph closure on the smallest sentence:** Most chapters. The pattern is a long sensory paragraph closed by a four-to-eight-word sentence that lands the weight.
- **Dialogue cadence:** declarative, often verbless responses, names used as sentences. Example: [03-hogganvik.md:15-23](../../content/en/03-hogganvik.md#L15-L23).
- **The drawer device:** physical-as-metaphor; introduced [03-hogganvik.md:155-173](../../content/en/03-hogganvik.md#L155-L173); literalized at workbench in [23-letter-from-shields.md:99-107](../../content/en/23-letter-from-shields.md#L99-L107).
