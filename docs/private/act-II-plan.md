## Open Act-level concerns from final reads

From ChatGPT (Act I final read) and Claude.ai (per-chapter):

1. **Karsten activation** — must move from signal-to-signal to signal-to-response. Plan: Ch 10 graveside-of-the-sea retrieval (avoidance / displacement); Ch 12 Haakon-POV obliquely registers what Olav has displaced. Not explicit. Movement, not explanation.
2. **Gjermund pressure** — the whole act, but specifically Ch 13 (incompetence as danger), Ch 14 (cruelty as direct attack on Olav), Ch 16 (defeat as crew-victory). Three-stage escalation.
3. **Sea-incident under stress** — Eliasson in Ch 10. Early enough to satisfy the Act I unmet-promise; severe enough to register as the trilogy's first true ship-stress.
4. **Female voice / Olava landing** — Olava is held for Act III per outline. Act II's only female-voice presence: the captain's wife in absence (referenced in Ch 9, Ch 11, Ch 16). Aunt's Sunday-night light as off-stage continuity. **Risk:** Act II is a forecastle book by structural necessity. Mitigation: hold the captain's wife's pre-voyage warning as a thread the reader hears in her absence; let her absence speak.

## Voice-register notes

- **Olav-POV chapters (Ch 9, 10, 11, 13, 14, 15, 16):** continue Scandinavian-plain → Miller-adjacent (per [voice-register.md] and memory). The tar-protest beat in Ch 14 is the act's tonal center; nothing in the surrounding prose competes for register-weight.

Mirror of Ch 5 Jens-POV in Act I — the act's emotional anchor, single chapter, register-distinct, structurally privileged.

## Old-prose conceptual harvest

Old prose is thinner for this stretch — most of the OLD/ folder ends around the *Sigrid* return. Concepts to consider:

- **The *Asta*-as-cruel-ship motif** — the name (love) against the captain (cruelty) against the figurehead (no face). Already in names-index. Carry through Act II as held thematic.
- **Captain's smile as rigging** — established in Ch 6 dinner / Ch 8 quay. Act II breaks the rigging deliberately by Ch 9 or Ch 10.

**Style pitfalls (carry forward from Act I plan):**
- Do not write Eliasson's death loud. Memoir's "Please, captain, do not jerk my foot so hard" is the loudest sentence; everything around it should be quieter than it.
- Do not write Haakon's recognition with hand-on-wrist intimacy in Act II (the old-prose Ch 1 mistake). The recognition is structural, not gestural, in Book I.
- Do not use the customhouse confrontation as a thesis-statement scene. Olav's outburst is inside the action; the meaning is in what Stensøy does next, not in Olav's lines.
