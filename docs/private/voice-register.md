# Voice Register — Per-POV Mapping

**Status:** Locked. Ken 2026-04-21 decision. Multi-register voice design for the restarted trilogy.

## Core principle

Each POV character has a distinct, period-authentic voice. All registers stay within 19th-century-literary-English-compatible range — no contemporary idiom, no anachronistic emotional vocabulary, no modern-American banter. Reader should hear whose chapter it is from the first paragraph without needing the label.

**What this is NOT:** contemporary-voice-in-period-setting (Hamilton-style anachronism). That was considered and rejected. Every POV stays period-authentic.

## Character-to-register mapping

### Olav Hestby — core protagonist POV
- **Register:** Madeline Miller
- **Primary comp:** *Song of Achilles*, *Circe*
- **Characteristics:** Close third-person, lyrical restraint, body-noticing as queer-awakening mechanism, period-authentic diction without being mannered, reserved at intimacy, peaks at emotional recognition
- **Note:** Miller writes first-person mythological; adapted here to close third for memoir-source grounding. Her lyric is pulled slightly toward Proulx (landscape, working-class physicality) to ground her in Norwegian maritime rather than Greek myth.

### Olava Lindøy — primary secondary POV
- **Register:** Colm Tóibín
- **Primary comp:** *Nora Webster*, *Brooklyn*
- **Characteristics:** Precise, domestic, documentary, measured. Ledger-keeping mind, careful observation, quiet psychological depth. Interior register of a literate rural Norwegian woman tracking her household, her correspondence, her marriage's architecture.

### Haakon Berg — secondary POV (Book I crew relationships, Books II/III callback)
- **Register:** Annie Proulx
- **Primary comp:** *Brokeback Mountain* (novella), *The Shipping News*
- **Characteristics:** Spare, working-class masculinity, physical-labor cadence. Shorter sentences, concrete nouns, less interior monologue, more what-he-sees-and-does. Maritime/rural register. Reserved about feeling; feelings carried in gesture and landscape.

### Anders Lindqvist — tertiary POV (Book II Copenhagen, Book III callback)
- **Register:** Joseph Conrad (early narrators)
- **Primary comp:** *The Secret Sharer*, *Heart of Darkness* (framing narrators)
- **Characteristics:** Careful European-English, slightly formal, precise. Non-native-inflected register for a Swedish clerk writing in or translated from a non-English mother tongue. Professional, reserved, periodically lyrical at specific moments.

### Captain Thoresen — tertiary POV (Book III Brooklyn)
- **Register:** Ernest Hemingway
- **Primary comp:** *In Our Time*, *Men Without Women*
- **Characteristics:** Terse authority, no lyric, no interior emotional vocabulary. Older captain register — professional, hard-won, austere. Short declarative sentences. Action and observation over reflection.

## Secondary POVs that may or may not be used (TBD at outlining)

- **Jens Hestby (father)** — if given POV. Register Proulx-adjacent, older generation, quieter.

## Verification rules

Before drafting any POV chapter:
1. Re-read the 2-3 sample paragraphs for that register (TBD — will be added as the voice-target.md work progresses)
2. Check: does the opening sentence land in the right register for the character?
3. After drafting: read the chapter aloud. Does it sound like the intended voice, or has it drifted toward default narrator?
