## Part 2 — Expansion zones for Book I

### Zone A: Gjermund-damage scene (expansion #2)
**Target payoff:** Book III Ch 11:46 carpenter-marlinspike reference.
**Required elements:**
- One of Gjermund's hands (a specific hand — give him a name)
- At the ship's waist, around the fo'c's'le or the mainmast
- Unsteady walk, bottle-drunk, daytime
- Attacks the carpenter with a marlinspike, taking it across the ribs
- Olav is at the foretop, sees the intention, cannot call alarm in time
- The carpenter survives

**Placement:** Between current Book I Ch 5 (Lisbon) and Ch 6 (the Becket), or in the voyage stretch after Lisbon before Wilmington. New chapter: "The Carpenter" — ~3-5K words.

**Book III Ch 11 implications:** The existing reference stands. No change needed there — it's correctly set up as "I saw this once, it taught me something about bodies," and the new Book I scene pays it off.

### Zone B: Haakon's "inside of the arc" lesson (if not already staged)
**Target payoff:** Book III Ch 11:86, 224 — explicit lesson on second day.
**Required elements:**
- Olav's SECOND day on the Sigrid
- Haakon explains: a swung weapon has full force at the end of the arc, no force at the inside; to survive a swung blow, step inside
- Demonstrated physically, or as commentary after a near-miss

**Placement:** If not already in Book I Ch 4 (Haakon's Watch), add to that chapter or Ch 3 (Hogganvik). Verify first.

**Action:** Read Ch 4 in full to confirm.

### Zone C: Thomas arc deepening (expansion #1 — the biggest)
**Target payoff:** Book III Ch 29 graveside reveal earning its 62-year weight.
**Required new material:**
- Thomas at the Bowery lodging — specific rituals of their shared year
- Thomas teaching Olav the "Vaageland" posture (not just the name — the walk, the voice)
- One explicit intimate-adjacent beat between Olav and Thomas (lee-of-galley analogue — the Bowery corner, the Sunday morning at the window)
- Thomas's presence at the tar-barrel at Wilmington staged as a specific scene (pays off II Ch 14:226)
- **The disappearance:** Friday morning of third week at Mrs Hovland's, milk-errand, the not-coming-back, the emptiness of Olav's waiting
- Olav's first 48 hours after Thomas disappears
- Olav's first aborted inquiry at the Wilmington waterfront the following week

**Placement:** Expand Ch 9 (Boat at Midnight) and Ch 10 (Vaageland) and Ch 11 (A Cough), OR insert 1-2 new chapters between Ch 10 and Ch 11.

**Coordination with Book II/III:**
- Book II Ch 14:226 ("Thomas Engelstad's face at the tar-barrel at Wilmington") — now pays off a specific Book I scene. Consider expanding the Ch 14 beat by one sentence to name what Thomas's face was doing at the tar-barrel (e.g., laughing, or not laughing; catching Olav's eye or not).
- Book II Ch 6:124, 126 (Wilmington reminiscence at Cape Town) — consider adding ~100 words where Olav thinks about Thomas specifically — the not-knowing where Thomas went — as one of the things the Cape Town signature does not carry.
- Book II Ch 18:232 (Olav's list to Sven in the dark) — "the hand on his wrist at the foretop of the *Sigrid* in 1876 and the man who had put it there" — consider whether Olav also names Thomas in that list, or whether Thomas is conspicuously absent (the not-naming being the form). Probably absent — preserves the form.
- Book III Ch 29 — no change needed. The expanded Book I earns the graveside beat.
